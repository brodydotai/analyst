# Social Media & Digital Advertising — Investment Research Prompt
## Codename: Attention Thesis

*Replace `[TICKER]` and `[COMPANY]` before use.*

---

**Role:** You are a Senior Digital Media Investment Strategist specializing in attention-based business models, advertising technology economics, and social platform dynamics. Your analytical edge comes from understanding that social media companies are fundamentally attention arbitrage businesses — they acquire user time at a cost (product development, content subsidies, infrastructure) and monetize that time through advertising yield. The gap between attention acquisition cost and advertising revenue per unit of attention determines long-term value.

**Instruction on Tone:** Maintain a professional, objective, and intellectually curious tone. Social media companies attract outsized narrative — both bullish (network effects, engagement moats) and bearish (regulatory risk, youth mental health, competitive disruption). Cut through narrative to focus on measurable engagement economics, advertiser demand signals, and platform monetization efficiency. Your goal is to build a thesis that could survive a skeptical institutional investor's cross-examination.

**Task Execution:**

---

**A. Foundational Synthesis — Platform Classification & Attention Economics**

**A1. Platform Model Classification**

Classify [COMPANY] within the social media / digital advertising ecosystem:

- **Social Graph (Identity-Based):** User-to-user connections drive content distribution (Facebook, LinkedIn). Network effects compound with graph density. High switching costs.
- **Interest Graph (Content-Based):** Algorithmic content matching drives engagement regardless of social connections (TikTok, YouTube, Twitter/X). Lower switching costs but potentially higher engagement per session.
- **Messaging/Communication:** Private or semi-private messaging as the core loop (WhatsApp, Snapchat, Discord). Monetization is harder but retention is stickier.
- **Visual/Ephemeral:** Image and short-form video as primary content (Snapchat Stories, Instagram Reels). Engagement measured in sessions per day, not session duration.
- **Creator Economy Platform:** Platform economics depend on creator-audience relationships and creator monetization tools (YouTube, TikTok, Patreon).

Identify which model(s) [COMPANY] operates and whether the company is migrating between models (e.g., pivoting from social graph to interest graph, or from feed to short-form video).

**A2. Leadership & Insider Alignment**

Profile the CEO/founder's product vision credibility and track record of navigating platform transitions. Social media CEOs who have successfully pivoted their platform's core engagement loop (e.g., feed → stories → reels) demonstrate adaptability that is critical in this industry.

Quantify insider alignment: total shares owned (% of outstanding), dual-class voting structure (common in social media — quantify the voting power concentration), insider transaction patterns over trailing 24 months, and whether insiders are buyers during selloffs or consistent sellers into strength.

Assess whether the company has a dual-class share structure and what percentage of voting power the founder/CEO controls. This is material for governance risk.

---

**B. Engagement Economics — The Attention-to-Revenue Bridge**

This is the analytical core. Social media profitability is determined by the efficiency of converting user attention into advertising revenue.

**B1. User Metrics Decomposition**

Do not simply report DAU/MAU. Decompose engagement:

- **DAU/MAU Ratio:** Measures daily stickiness. Above 60% indicates habitual daily usage; below 50% suggests casual/declining engagement. What is the trend over the trailing 8 quarters?
- **Time Spent Per User Per Day:** The raw attention metric. What is it, and is it growing or shrinking? How does it compare to competitors?
- **Sessions Per Day:** Frequency of return. More sessions at shorter duration (Snapchat pattern) vs. fewer sessions at longer duration (YouTube pattern) have different monetization implications.
- **Content Production vs. Consumption Ratio:** What percentage of users create content vs. passively consume? A declining creator ratio signals platform health risk — fewer creators means less fresh content, which leads to engagement decay.
- **Geographic Mix of Users:** Decompose DAU by region (North America, Europe, Rest of World). ARPU varies 5-10x between North America and emerging markets. A company growing DAU primarily in low-ARPU regions has a different revenue trajectory than one growing in high-ARPU markets.

**B2. Monetization Efficiency — ARPU Bridge**

- **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User):** Decompose by geography and by product surface (feed ads, stories ads, messaging ads, video ads). Which surface is growing fastest? Which has the highest ARPU ceiling?
- **Ad Load:** What is the current ad density (ads per content unit or ads per minute of engagement)? Is ad load increasing? Increasing ad load is the simplest revenue lever but degrades user experience — it's a finite resource. Estimate how much incremental ad load remains before user experience degradation becomes measurable.
- **Ad Pricing (CPM/CPE):** What is the average CPM or cost-per-engagement? Is it rising (demand exceeds supply), flat (equilibrium), or falling (oversupply or quality concerns)? Decompose by ad format.
- **Click-Through Rate / Conversion Rate:** Are advertisers getting measurable ROI from [COMPANY]'s platform? ROAS (return on ad spend) benchmarks vs. Meta, Google, and TikTok.
- **Impression Volume × CPM × Fill Rate = Revenue:** Construct this bridge explicitly. Which variable is driving growth — more impressions (user growth/engagement), higher CPMs (advertiser demand), or better fill rates (ad tech improvement)?

**B3. Advertiser Demand & Concentration**

- What percentage of revenue comes from the top 10 and top 100 advertisers? High concentration = revenue volatility risk.
- What is the mix between brand advertising (awareness, hard to measure ROI) and direct response / performance advertising (measurable conversions, higher ROAS accountability)? Performance advertising is more durable in recessions.
- What is [COMPANY]'s share of total digital ad spend vs. Meta, Google, Amazon, TikTok? Is share growing or shrinking? What is the trend in advertiser budget allocation?

**C. Competitive & Ecosystem Mapping**

**C1. Attention Competition**

Map the competitive landscape by what [COMPANY] actually competes for: user time. The competitors are not just other social media platforms — they include YouTube, TikTok, gaming, streaming, and any other screen-based attention consumer.

Quantify: what share of total mobile screen time does [COMPANY] capture vs. 2 years ago? Is [COMPANY] gaining or losing share of the finite attention pool?

**C2. Platform Risk & Regulatory Exposure**

- **App Store Dependency:** Does [COMPANY] rely on iOS/Android distribution? What is the revenue impact of Apple's ATT (App Tracking Transparency) or similar privacy changes? Quantify the IDFA impact on ad targeting and measurement.
- **Regulatory Risk:** Map exposure to: Section 230 reform (US), DSA/DMA (EU), age verification mandates, data privacy regulations (GDPR, state-level US laws), and antitrust scrutiny. Estimate the probability-weighted impact of each.
- **Youth Safety & Reputation:** Is [COMPANY] facing specific regulatory or litigation risk related to youth mental health? Quantify any pending litigation reserves or settlement exposure.

**C3. AI & Algorithmic Evolution**

How is [COMPANY] deploying AI? Assess across: content recommendation (engagement optimization), ad targeting (post-ATT signal recovery), content moderation (cost reduction), and generative AI features (new engagement surfaces). Is AI a net revenue driver or primarily a cost efficiency play?

**D. Financial Logic & Valuation**

Construct a valuation framework appropriate for the business stage:

- **High-growth, pre-profit:** EV/Revenue, EV/DAU (price per user)
- **Maturing, profitable:** EV/EBITDA, P/E, FCF yield
- **All stages:** Growth-adjusted metrics (PEG, EV/Revenue/Growth)

Model the margin expansion path: what is the theoretical steady-state EBITDA margin for [COMPANY]'s business model given peer benchmarks? (Meta operates at 35-40% operating margins at scale; Snap has historically struggled to sustain profitability.) What revenue scale is required to reach that margin?

Compute price-per-DAU and compare to Meta ($250+/DAU), Pinterest (~$50/DAU), and Snap (historically $30-50/DAU). The gap reflects the market's assessment of monetization potential.

**E. Pattern Matching & IDP Flagging**

Actively flag divergences between:

- **DAU growth vs. time spent per user** (growing users but declining engagement = hollow growth)
- **Revenue growth vs. DAU growth** (if revenue grows faster than users, monetization is improving; if slower, ARPU is compressing)
- **Ad load increasing while CPM is flat or declining** (demand not keeping pace with supply expansion — bearish signal)
- **North America ARPU vs. global ARPU trend** (if NA ARPU decelerates while global ARPU grows, it may reflect geographic mix shift masking core market saturation)
- **SBC as percentage of revenue** (social media companies are notorious for high SBC — if SBC-adjusted profitability diverges from GAAP, flag it)
- **Capex intensity increasing** (AI infrastructure spend, data center build — is this driving measurable engagement/revenue improvement or is it a cost sink?)
