## A. Foundational Synthesis — Material Classification & Value Chain Position

### Material Classification

Kennametal operates in the **hard materials category**, specifically engineered tungsten carbide (cemented carbide) and advanced ceramics. These materials occupy a critical position in global manufacturing:

- **Tungsten Carbide:** A composite of tungsten carbide particles in a cobalt binder matrix. Hardness: 1000–1200 HV (Vickers hardness). Superior to high-speed steel by 3–4 orders of magnitude in cutting edge durability.
- **Ceramics:** Alumina and mixed-ceramic compositions for high-temperature, high-speed applications.
- **Application-Specific Composites:** Engineered for metal cutting, mining, construction, and defense applications.

### Value Chain Position

Kennametal operates across three critical value chain nodes:

1. **Raw Material Sourcing → Powder Production:** Owns tungsten ore sourcing relationships and powder metallurgy capabilities. Tungsten is procured from a concentrated supplier base (China dominates 85%+ of refined production). This upstream positioning provides margin leverage during commodity appreciation.

2. **Toll Formulation & Cementation:** Proprietary cementation and sintering processes create application-tailored hard material systems. High switching costs for OEM customers—tooling qualification cycles run 12–24 months.

3. **Application-Engineered Distribution:** Dual-channel distribution (direct to large OEMs like aerospace suppliers; WIDIA brand for SME/industrial distributors) ensures reach across end markets while maintaining premium-pricing capacity.

**Competitive Moat Anchors:** Kennametal's value chain position is reinforced by:
- Proprietary material science (decades of R&D in tungsten carbide formulations)
- Qualification lock-in (switching costs in OEM aerospace/defense applications)
- Scale economies in powder production (fixed cost absorption)
- Tungsten supply-chain relationships (long-term agreements with miners/concentrators)

## B. Materials Economics — The Specification-to-Revenue Bridge

### Tungsten Commodity Dynamics & Margin Cascade

Tungsten prices exhibit extreme cyclicality, yet Kennametal's margin structure is partially de-coupled from spot volatility through:

1. **Tungsten Price Inflation (Direct Driver):**
   - YoY increase: +190% (as of December 2025)
   - Since November 2025: +47%
   - Current market environment: Acute supply deficit driven by Chinese refinery maintenance, geopolitical supply concerns, and accelerating demand from defense/aerospace sectors
   - Impact on Kennametal: Q2 FY2026 benefited from $17M favorable price/raw material effect in Infrastructure segment

2. **Pricing Pass-Through Mechanics:**
   - **Raw Material Surcharge Model:** Kennametal deployed a mid-single-digit price increase effective January 1, 2026. This surcharge mechanism allows real-time adjustment to tungsten spot prices without customer renegotiation friction.
   - **Timing Advantage:** In Q1 and Q2 FY2026, the company captured pricing ahead of cost realization, creating a favorable working capital and earnings sequence. Management guides this positive spread to extend through Q3, turning neutral in Q4.
   - **FY2026 Revenue Bridge:** Of the +11% net price/tariff contribution in FY2026 guidance, approximately 8–10 percentage points is attributable to tungsten/raw material surcharges.

3. **Specification Economics & Mix:**
   - **Aerospace & Defense Premium:** Highest-margin business (typically 35%+ gross margin at segment operating level). Q2 growth of +23% constant-currency organic growth is a meaningful margin-accretive mix shift.
   - **Earthworks (Infrastructure):** Mining and construction tools. Benefited from +18% organic growth in Q2, driven by commodity cycle-driven capex recovery.
   - **General Engineering:** The core metal cutting market, growing +8%. Lower margin profile but large addressable market.

4. **Cost Structure & Leverage:**
   - Manufacturing costs (labor, energy, overhead) are largely fixed over quarterly/annual scales
   - Tungsten and raw material costs are variable and commodity-linked
   - As tungsten prices remain elevated, gross margin % can inflate even with modest revenue growth due to operating leverage
   - Q2 FY2026 adjusted EBITDA margin of 17.1% (vs. 13.9% YoY) evidences this leverage

### Working Capital Implications

Tungsten price appreciation has inflated inventory: +$85M to $690M total inventory. This represents a $97M net working capital increase YoY. However:
- Inventory is marked at higher cost basis (elevated tungsten prices)
- Customers are prepaying or accelerating orders in response to tungsten scarcity ("buy-ahead" behavior)
- This creates a favorable cash timing sequence: revenue recognition precedes cash outlay
- Q2 FY2026 showed net income recovery (+89% YoY to $33.9M), evidence of margin expansion outpacing working capital drag

## E. Supply Chain & Geopolitical Risk Architecture

### Tungsten Supply Chain Fragility

**Current Supply Deficit Drivers:**

1. **Chinese Refinery Maintenance:** Primary tungsten refineries (Sino-Tungsten, China Tungsten) have announced maintenance windows extending into Q1–Q2 2026, constraining refined tungsten ore availability.

2. **Geopolitical Supply Risk:** Tungsten is classified as a critical mineral by the U.S. Department of Energy. China controls 85%+ of refined tungsten production. Escalating U.S.–China tensions create:
   - Potential export restrictions on tungsten concentrates (precedent: rare earths 2010–2011)
   - Strategic stockpiling by defense contractors (increasing demand)
   - Supply diversification initiatives (e.g., Wolfram Mining, Peru; Almonty Industries, Portugal/South Korea)

3. **Mine-Level Constraints:** Global primary tungsten production (concentrated in China, Peru, Russia, Canada) is not expanding rapidly. Capital intensity of greenfield mining (5–7 year lead times) limits supply response.

**Impact on Kennametal:**

- **Favorable (Near-term, 12–18 months):** Contracted supply agreements and long-standing miner relationships position Kennametal to secure tungsten at discount to spot prices. This amplifies margin upside vs. competitors without supply security.
- **Unfavorable (Long-term, 2+ years):** If Chinese export restrictions materialize, cost inflation would hurt all Western producers. Kennametal would need to source from non-Chinese refinery capacity (limited availability, higher cost).
- **Mitigation:** Company's cost reduction program includes supply chain redundancy; FY2027 targets likely incorporate secondary tungsten recycling and non-Chinese sourcing mix increase.

### Cobalt & Secondary Rare Earth Dependencies

Cobalt is the secondary binder metal in cemented carbide (tungsten carbide + cobalt). Cobalt supply is concentrated in Democratic Republic of Congo (65% global production). While less volatile than tungsten, cobalt prices are subject to mine-level disruption.

**Kennametal's Exposure:** Modest. Cobalt is typically 5–8% of total hard material cost. Current focus on tungsten surcharge pricing suggests company is managing cobalt separately (likely contracted).

### Supply Chain Resilience Rating

| Risk Factor | Severity | Kennametal Mitigation |
|------------|----------|----------------------|
| Tungsten supply deficit | High (12–18 months) | Long-term agreements; supply diversity |
| Chinese export risk | Medium (2+ year horizon) | Secondary sourcing; recycling programs |
| Cobalt volatility | Low–Medium | Contracted supply; modest cost impact |
| Manufacturing concentration | Medium | Consolidation/automation program |

**Overall Supply Chain Risk Rating: 6/10 (Moderate).** Kennametal has defensible near-term positioning but faces long-term geopolitical headwinds common to the specialty materials sector.

### Innovation Themes (FY2026–FY2027)

**Theme 1: Coating Innovation for Aerospace**
- Advanced PVD/CVD coatings reducing tool wear by 10–15% over baseline
- Enables higher cutting speeds; reduces tool changeout frequency
- Benefit: Premium pricing (3–5% above uncoated) + OEM loyalty deepening
- Status: In qualification with major airframers (12–18 month cycles)

**Theme 2: Tungsten Carbide-Composite Formulations**
- Mixed-media carbides balancing hardness/toughness for interrupted cuts
- Reduces tool breakage; extends tool life
- Particularly valuable in automotive (roboticized stamping, unpredictable cut zones)
- Status: Early commercialization; ramp expected FY2027

**Theme 3: Recycled Tungsten Carbide Integration**
- Secondary tungsten sourcing from scrap tool recycling
- Reduces raw material cost by 15–20% for non-premium grades
- Supports sustainability narrative; unlocks margin on recycled-grade tooling
- Status: Pilot programs active; full ramp FY2027–2028

**Theme 4: Defense-Specific Tungsten Solutions**
- Custom tungsten penetrators, armor composites for ballistic applications
- Capitalize on defense budget growth and supply chain de-risking
- Status: Active discussions with prime contractors; classified project funnel

**Assessment:** Innovation roadmap is incrementally focused (coating, composite) rather than transformational. However, positioning in A&D is strategically sound. Pipeline maturity: Moderate (0.65). Expect 2–3% incremental margin upside from new products over 24–36 months.

## H. High-Agency Investigation Tracks

### Investigation Track 1: Tungsten Price Structural vs. Cyclical

**Question:** Is the 190% YoY tungsten price increase a temporary commodity cycle or a structural repricing?

**Evidence Supporting Structural:**
- Chinese refinery maintenance is extending into H1 2026; suggests supply tightness not rapidly reversing
- Defense spending environment (U.S., NATO) is structurally supportive of tungsten demand
- Supply diversification (non-Chinese) will take years; Chinese capacity remains constrained
- Geopolitical fragmentation reducing fungibility of tungsten spot markets

**Evidence Supporting Cyclical:**
- Historical tungsten cycles (2008–2011, 2017–2018) show 50%+ reversions from peaks within 18–24 months
- Secondary tungsten recycling (Kennametal's innovation track) could accelerate supply growth
- If global growth slows sharply (recession risk), metal demand falls; tungsten demand follows
- Current spot prices ($400–450/mtu) remain historically high but not at peaks

**Analyst Assessment:** Most likely scenario is **50% structural, 50% cyclical**. Tungsten will decline from current peaks but plateau at 20–30% above pre-2024 baseline. This implies FY2027–2028 EPS accretion of ~$0.15–0.25/share vs. our normalized earnings estimate.

**Investigation Required:** Monitor Q3 FY2026 earnings call for management's tungsten price assumptions in FY2027 guidance.

---

### Investigation Track 2: Cost Program Credibility & Execution Risk

**Question:** Can Kennametal deliver the full $125M cost reduction program by end FY2027 without compromising margin quality (e.g., via reduced R&D, customer service degradation)?

**Evidence Supporting Credibility:**
- Q2 FY2026 delivered $8M in restructuring savings on schedule
- Program is already 52% of the way to $125M target ($65M annualized run-rate)
- Actions are largely structural (plant consolidation, automation, overhead reduction) — not temporary cost-cutting
- Company has a history of executing cost programs (2015–2018 restructurings were successful)

**Evidence Supporting Risk:**
- Manufacturing consolidation can disrupt customer service if execution is poor
- Automation capex requirements may squeeze free cash flow in FY2026–2027
- Labor relations in consolidated plants (potential union friction, workforce transition)
- If demand softens unexpectedly, fixed cost base reduction may be insufficient

**Analyst Assessment:** **Cost program is 75% credible.** Management has track record and early delivery. However, watch for Q3 FY2026 execution updates and FY2027 capex guidance.

**Investigation Required:** Request management detail on specific plant consolidation timelines and automation capex requirements in Q3 FY2026 earnings call.

### Investigation Track 3: Competitive Positioning Sustainability vs. Sandvik & OSG

**Question:** Will Kennametal's current margin advantage (driven by tungsten pricing and cost program) persist against competitors, or will Sandvik/OSG retaliate with pricing pressure and increased R&D?

**Competitive Context:**
- Sandvik Coromant is larger ($1.8–2.0B metal cutting revenue), more diversified globally
- OSG (Osaka Seiko) has lower cost structure (Japan-based, manufacturing scale)
- Both competitors are raising prices in response to tungsten (not unique to KMT)

**Evidence Supporting KMT Sustainability:**
- A&D market is less price-competitive; OEM lock-in favors KMT
- KMT's supply chain positioning (tungsten access) is differentiated
- Cost program is progressing faster than peer restructurings (typical 2–3 year cycles)

**Evidence Supporting Competitive Retaliation:**
- Sandvik has deeper financial resources for aggressive pricing/R&D
- OSG has structural cost advantage in Japan (labor, manufacturing)
- Meta-competition in coatings and composite tooling could erode KMT's innovation premium

**Analyst Assessment:** **KMT has 2–3 year window of margin superiority.** By FY2028, expect mean reversion as peers execute similar cost programs. Implication: Current valuation upside is realistic only if investors believe cost program creates durable ROIC advantage or if tungsten pricing persists longer than expected.

**Investigation Required:** Conduct detailed benchmarking of KMT cost structure vs. Sandvik/OSG in Q3 FY2026 call; assess sustainability of 17%+ EBITDA margins into FY2027.

### Bear Perspective: "Cyclical Commodity Play Masquerading as Operational Story"

The bear case on Kennametal is straightforward: the company is experiencing a temporary tungsten commodity windfall while simultaneously executing cost reductions, creating an illusion of transformational earnings growth. Strip out the tungsten benefit (~$100M+ implied EBITDA contribution in FY2026 vs. FY2025 baseline), and Kennametal's operational earnings growth is mid-single-digit at best. The cost program is real but incremental, delivering 150–200 bps of structural margin expansion—not the multi-year transformation that justifies a revaluation multiple.

**Bear Case Narrative:**

Tungsten prices are reverting from unsustainable levels. Historical tungsten cycles (2008–2011, 2017–2018) show 40–60% price reversions from peaks within 18–24 months. Secondary tungsten recycling, non-Chinese refinery capacity expansion, and potential demand destruction from economic slowdown will normalize pricing within 24–36 months. Kennametal's FY2026 guidance assumes tungsten surcharges in the mid-single-digit range (+2–3% incremental), but even at current elevated prices, this severely underestimates the tungsten contribution. If tungsten reverts to pre-2024 levels, Kennametal's FY2027 EPS would face a -$0.20 to -$0.30 headwind, bringing "normalized" earnings to ~$1.50 adjusted EPS (vs. guidance midpoint of $2.25 reported).

The cost program, while credible in early execution, is not sufficient to justify valuation rerating. $125M of cost savings on a $2.2B sales base = 5.7% structural improvement, which improves ROIC but does not transform competitive positioning relative to Sandvik or OSG. Manufacturing consolidation has execution risk; automation capex will require $50–80M over 2–3 years, pressuring free cash flow and limiting shareholder returns.

The A&D growth story is already priced in. Kennametal's +23% A&D organic growth reflects share gains in a high-qualification market, but the addressable market for metal cutting in aerospace is relatively small (~$2–3B globally). Saturation is a near-term risk; moreover, A&D customers are cyclically exposed to commercial aircraft builds and defense budget appropriations, both subject to reversion risk.

The valuation discount (6.8x EV/EBITDA) is not a screaming bargain; it is a reflection of the cyclical upside. Kennametal has not earned above 8.5x EV/EBITDA multiples historically outside of peak cycle periods. Current 6.8x is appropriate for a tungsten-dependent earnings cycle.

**Bear Target: $35–40 (24-month horizon).** Conviction: 0.55.

### Macro Perspective: "Specialty Materials as Inflation Hedge & Capital Cycle Play"

The macro perspective on Kennametal is nuanced, viewing the company not as a pure tungsten commodity play but as a specialty materials proxy within a broader industrial capital cycle recovery.

**Macro Narrative:**

From a macro lens, Kennametal is attractive if (1) inflation expectations remain elevated (tungsten prices are an inflation hedge), (2) government capex cycles support infrastructure and defense investment, and (3) manufacturing reshoring/de-risking trends favor supply-chain-resilient producers. Conversely, Kennametal is vulnerable if (1) disinflationary pressures mount (reducing commodity pricing power), (2) recession risks materialize (reducing end-market demand across A&D, energy, earthworks), and (3) Chinese supply capacity comes online aggressively (global tungsten oversupply).

The company benefits from two secular macro trends: (1) defense spending growth (NATO expansion, U.S.–China competition) and (2) energy transition infrastructure (renewable energy capex, grid modernization). Both trends are multi-year and relatively insulated from near-term cyclicality. However, both trends are reflected in the +23% A&D growth and +4% Energy growth already realized in Q2.

The macro risk is recession. Kennametal's end markets (General Engineering, Transportation, Mining) are cyclically sensitive. A 10–15% demand contraction in a recession would compress earnings by 20–30%. However, current market consensus does not price significant recession risk into commodity prices or industrial valuations, suggesting consensus is constructive on macro stability.

**Macro Recommendation:** Kennametal is a reasonable tactical position (6–12 month horizon) within a growth-supportive macro environment (3–4% nominal growth, stable inflation, continued defense capex). For investors with higher recession probability estimates (>25% 12-month odds), reduce position size or implement downside hedges.
