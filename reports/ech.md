## Executive Summary

ECH is the iShares MSCI Chile ETF, tracking the MSCI Chile IMI 25-50 Index to provide broad exposure to Chilean equities. The ETF trades at ~$41.56 with AUM of $1.18B, a 0.59% expense ratio, and a 2.6% dividend yield. The P/E ratio is 17.85x. Top holdings are SQM (13.4% — lithium and specialty chemicals), Banco de Chile (11.4%), LATAM Airlines (10.8%), Falabella (5.2%), and Parque Arauco (5.1%). Sector allocation is weighted toward financials (25.8%), basic materials (17.8%), industrials (16.4%), consumer cyclical (12.3%), and utilities (12.1%). The 52-week range of $26.32-$47.85 reflects significant volatility driven by copper price swings and Chilean domestic policy uncertainty. ECH offers leveraged exposure to the copper supercycle through Chile's position as the world's largest copper producer (~25% of global output), while also providing diversified access to Chilean banking, retail, and infrastructure.

## Macro Context

As of March 2026, Chile's economy is growing at 2-3% GDP with copper prices near record highs (~$12,000/mt) providing a powerful terms-of-trade tailwind. The central bank is pursuing cautious monetary easing after inflation peaked at 4.5% in 2025 from electricity tariff adjustments, with inflation projected to converge to the 3% target in 2026. Fiscal consolidation is underway, and rising real wages are supporting domestic consumption. However, governance gridlock continues to stall key reforms in taxation, pensions, and lithium policy — the government's attempt to increase state involvement in lithium production has created uncertainty for SQM and the broader mining sector. Chile remains highly sensitive to Chinese demand fluctuations (China and the EU together consume the bulk of Chilean mineral exports). The peso has stabilized but remains vulnerable to copper price reversals or EM risk-off events.

## Research

### 1. Business Overview & Competitive Position

ECH provides the most liquid U.S.-listed vehicle for Chilean equity exposure. The fund tracks the MSCI Chile IMI 25-50 Index, which represents the Chilean investable market across large, mid, and small-cap segments with a 25/50 capping methodology to limit concentration. Chile's equity market is dominated by five sectors: financials (banks benefiting from rate cuts and credit growth), basic materials (copper and lithium miners), industrials (airlines, infrastructure), consumer cyclical (retail, real estate), and utilities (power generation and distribution). The revenue model for the underlying companies is heavily commodity-linked — even Chilean banks derive significant credit growth from mining sector activity. SQM's dominant 13.4% weight means ECH's performance is substantially driven by lithium and specialty chemical prices. LATAM Airlines' 10.8% weight adds idiosyncratic airline exposure (recovery from post-pandemic restructuring). ([Source: iShares ECH](https://www.ishares.com/us/products/239618/ishares-msci-chile-capped-etf))

### 2. Copper & Lithium Exposure

Chile produced approximately 5.3 million metric tons of copper in 2024, representing ~25% of global mine output. The country's copper output directly impacts government revenue (copper royalties and Codelco state mining company dividends), bank loan quality (mining sector credit), and ancillary services (transport, energy, equipment). ECH captures this copper beta through both direct mining holdings and indirect economic linkages across the portfolio. SQM, the fund's largest holding, is one of the world's largest lithium producers from Chile's Atacama salt flat — the world's highest-grade lithium brine resource. Lithium carbonate prices have been volatile but remain elevated above pre-2020 levels, and EV adoption continues to drive structural demand growth. Chile also holds the world's largest lithium reserves, but government lithium policy uncertainty (proposals to increase state control) has weighed on SQM's valuation. ([Source: DevDiscourse](https://www.devdiscourse.com/article/headlines/3733555-chiles-economic-outlook-growth-and-copper-prices-drive-optimism-for-2026))

### 3. Macro & Monetary Policy

Chile's GDP is projected at 2-3% growth in 2026, driven by domestic demand recovery, rising real wages, and improving terms of trade from copper prices. The central bank is cautiously easing after inflation peaked at 4.5% in 2025. Rate cuts should benefit ECH's 25.8% financial services weight by improving credit growth and net interest margins for banks like Banco de Chile (11.4%) and Banco Santander Chile (4.4%). Fiscal consolidation is reducing government debt growth but limiting stimulus capacity. The OECD projects 2.2% growth in 2026-2027, while the IMF is slightly more conservative at 2.0%. The key domestic risk is policy gridlock: pension reform, tax reform, and lithium regulation have all stalled in Congress, creating a reform limbo that deters foreign investment. Chile's risk rating remains investment-grade, with manageable external debt and adequate reserves, but the political class's inability to pass structural reforms limits the upside re-rating potential. ([Source: OECD](https://www.oecd.org/en/topics/sub-issues/economic-surveys/chile-economic-snapshot.html))

### 4. Valuation & Fund Mechanics

ECH's P/E of 17.85x is moderate for an EM single-country ETF with heavy commodity exposure. For comparison, Brazil's EWZ trades at lower multiples (~8-10x) but with greater political risk, while Mexico's EWW trades at similar levels (~15-17x). The 2.6% dividend yield provides income while waiting for capital appreciation. The 0.59% expense ratio is standard for single-country EM ETFs. Key structural considerations: ECH is denominated in USD but holds peso-denominated assets, so currency movements directly impact returns. A strengthening peso (likely in a copper-bullish environment) amplifies returns; peso weakness drags on dollar-denominated performance. Liquidity is adequate for individual investors ($1.18B AUM) but could be limiting for institutional-size positions. The fund's concentration in SQM (13.4%) means investors are effectively making a significant bet on lithium alongside the broader Chilean economy. ([Source: ETF.com ECH](https://www.etf.com/ECH))
