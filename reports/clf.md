# Cleveland-Cliffs Inc. (CLF)

**Date:** 03/03/2026
**Playbook:** specialty-and-advanced-materials
**Sector:** Steel & Specialty Materials

---

## Executive Summary

Cleveland-Cliffs is the largest flat-rolled steel producer in North America, operating an integrated steelmaking platform spanning iron ore mining, blast furnace steelmaking, and downstream finishing across hot-rolled, cold-rolled, coated, plate, stainless, and electrical steel products. The stock trades at ~$10.57 with a market cap of ~$5.4B. Full-year 2025 revenues were $18.6B on 16.2M net tons shipped, but Adjusted EBITDA collapsed to $37M (vs. $773M in 2024) as HRC pricing weakness compressed margins. Q4 2025 saw a narrower Adjusted EBITDA loss of -$21M (vs. -$81M prior year). The company is heavily leveraged (~$4.5B net debt) but is positioned for a margin inflection in 2026: the termination of an unfavorable slab supply contract is expected to add ~$500M in annual EBITDA, 50% Section 232 tariffs have pushed domestic HRC to ~$980/st, and multi-year fixed-price auto contracts secure high-margin volumes.

---

## Macro Context

As of March 2026, the U.S. steel market is operating under a protective tariff umbrella — 50% Section 232 tariffs (raised from 25% in June 2025) have driven flat-rolled imports down 42% in 2025 and pushed domestic HRC pricing to ~$980/st, with Nucor implementing successive price increases in early 2026. Lead times are stretching to 4-8 weeks on robust backlogs from infrastructure, data center, and automotive demand. The macro setup is favorable for domestic producers: imports are at multi-year lows, domestic product is now ~$49/st cheaper than tariff-adjusted imports, and reshoring tailwinds are increasing capacity utilization. The key risk is global overcapacity — China produces over 50% of the world's steel and any demand slowdown could pressure global benchmarks even with tariff protection. Fed policy remains restrictive, which constrains construction and housing starts, but infrastructure spending (IIJA, CHIPS Act) provides an offsetting demand floor.

## Bull Case

1. Termination of the unfavorable slab supply contract adds an estimated $500M in annual EBITDA starting in 2026, while steel unit costs decline ~$10/nt — management calls this "the single largest margin catalyst in Cliffs' history."
2. Section 232 tariffs at 50% have crushed import competition (flat-rolled imports down 42%), supporting domestic HRC at ~$980/st and extending lead times to 4-8 weeks — a pricing environment that directly benefits CLF as the largest integrated domestic producer.
3. Multi-year fixed-price contracts with all major automotive OEMs lock in high-margin volumes at favorable terms, with CLF gaining market share as the dominant supplier of advanced high-strength steel (AHSS) for lightweighting in EVs and next-gen platforms.

## Bear Case

1. CLF carries ~$4.5B in net debt with near-term maturities, and 2025 Adjusted EBITDA of just $37M produced a leverage ratio exceeding 100x — any delay in the margin recovery or steel price reversal could create a liquidity crisis.
2. The blast furnace fleet is aging and capital-intensive vs. EAF competitors (Nucor, Steel Dynamics), and decarbonization pressure could accelerate the value gap — CLF's integrated model is an advantage in quality but a liability in cost flexibility.
3. Heavy automotive exposure (~40% of volumes) creates cyclical concentration risk — if U.S. auto production slows due to tariff-driven component cost inflation or consumer demand weakness, CLF's highest-margin segment contracts simultaneously.

## Research

### 1. Material Classification & Value Chain Position

Cleveland-Cliffs controls the most vertically integrated steelmaking platform in North America, from Great Lakes iron ore mines through blast furnace steelmaking to downstream finishing operations. The product mix is diversified: 40% hot-rolled, 28% coated (galvanized, galvannealed), 15% cold-rolled, 5% plate, 3% stainless and electrical, and 9% other (including slabs). The stainless and electrical steel segment — acquired through the 2024 Stelco acquisition and existing GOES (grain-oriented electrical steel) operations — represents the highest-specification, highest-margin product line. GOES is critical for transformer cores in grid infrastructure, and CLF is the only North American producer, creating a near-monopoly domestic position. The automotive franchise supplies advanced high-strength steel (AHSS) for lightweighting, crash management, and structural components, where material qualification cycles run 12-24 months and switching costs are prohibitive. This specification moat is CLF's most defensible competitive advantage. ([Source: Cleveland-Cliffs Q4 2025](https://www.clevelandcliffs.com/news/news-releases/detail/693/cleveland-cliffs-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2025))

### 2. Pricing Power & Tariff Environment

The 50% Section 232 tariff regime has fundamentally restructured the domestic steel pricing environment. Flat-rolled imports plunged 42% in 2025, with coated sheet imports hitting their lowest daily rate since December 2010. Domestic HRC is currently ~$980/st with Nucor implementing successive price increases in early 2026 — the spread between domestic and tariff-adjusted import prices has inverted, with U.S. product now ~$49/st cheaper than landed imports. This creates a pricing floor that insulates domestic mills from global overcapacity. For CLF specifically, the slab supply contract termination is transformative: management estimates a $700M revenue improvement at current prices and a $500M EBITDA uplift annually, as CLF shifts from purchasing external slabs at unfavorable terms to utilizing its own integrated capacity. Combined with ~$10/nt unit cost reductions, the margin bridge from 2025's $37M Adjusted EBITDA to a potential $1.5B+ in 2026 is the central bull case. ([Source: Steel Industry News](https://steelindustry.news/nucors-six-week-price-surge-what-it-signals-for-the-steel-market/))

### 3. Automotive & Specialty Steel Franchise

CLF's automotive franchise is the crown jewel of its product portfolio. The company has signed multi-year fixed-price contracts with all major automotive OEMs operating in North America, increasing market share and securing high-margin volumes through at least 2028. Automotive steel demands the highest specification grades — AHSS, ultra-high-strength steel (UHSS), and advanced coated products — where CLF's integrated quality control from iron ore through finishing provides a measurable advantage over EAF-based competitors processing scrap. The EV transition is a net positive for CLF: battery electric vehicles require ~30% more steel per vehicle than ICE equivalents (battery enclosures, structural reinforcement, crash management). Management describes the current order book as "robust" with rising lead times, and the combination of tariff protection and OEM reshoring commitments supports volume visibility into 2027. The risk is cyclical: if U.S. auto production contracts (due to tariff-driven component cost inflation or consumer demand weakness), CLF's highest-margin segment bears the brunt. ([Source: Cleveland-Cliffs Earnings Call](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/09/cleveland-cliffs-clf-q4-2025-earnings-transcript/))

### 4. Balance Sheet & Capital Structure

CLF's balance sheet is the primary risk factor. Net debt is approximately $4.5B against 2025 Adjusted EBITDA of just $37M — a technically unsustainable leverage ratio. However, the market is pricing forward normalization: if the slab contract termination delivers the guided $500M EBITDA uplift and HRC pricing holds near $1,000/st, 2026 Adjusted EBITDA could reach $1.5-2.0B, bringing net leverage to a manageable 2.3-3.0x. The company has near-term debt maturities that require refinancing or repayment, adding urgency to the EBITDA recovery. Asset sales (potential divestiture of non-core operations) and working capital optimization provide additional levers. Analyst consensus for 2026 EPS has been revised upward to $0.42 (from $0.06), reflecting the margin inflection thesis. The median analyst price target of $13.00 (range $8.90-$16.80) across 22 analysts reflects a consensus Hold rating — the market acknowledges the catalyst but demands proof of execution before re-rating. ([Source: Wall Street Zen](https://www.wallstreetzen.com/stocks/us/nyse/clf/stock-forecast))
