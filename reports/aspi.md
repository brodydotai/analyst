## Research

### 1. Fuel Cycle Classification & Strategic Importance

**Classification:** ASPI straddles two distinct nuclear fuel markets:
- **HALEU (High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium):** Uranium-235 enrichment for advanced reactor designs (TerraPower, others). Supply-critical due to Russian import ban; TerraPower supply contract (150 MT, 2028-2037) positions ASPI in fast-growing segment. DOE demand estimates 50 MT/year by 2035 vs. estimated US production today near zero.
- **Specialty Isotopes (Non-Fuel):** Silicon-28 (quantum computing), Molybdenum-100 (medical), others. Produced at commercial scale for first time in South Africa facilities (started Feb-March 2025). Non-substitutable in target applications; no competing US producer.

**Strategic Importance:** ASPI holds no radioactive materials license yet (QLE subsidiary pursuing at Pelindaba, South Africa). Current focus: isotope enrichment via ASP technology (aerodynamic separation, lower capex vs. centrifuge). Critical for US nuclear fuel independence and advanced reactor enablement.

### 2. Supply/Demand Fundamentals

**Supply Profile:**
- Two operational facilities (Pretoria): Carbon-14 (Feb 2025), Silicon-28 (March 2025). Both early-stage (< 2 months operational history).
- Projected revenue 2026-2027: $50-70M. LTM baseline: $8.38M. Implies 6-8x growth required for guidance midpoint.
- Capital: $114M cash + DFC-backed $500M Renergen acquisition financing. Sufficient for South African facility ramp but insufficient if US HALEU scale-up required faster.

**Demand Profile:**
- **Silicon-28:** 3 supply contracts signed Sept 2025; largest contract (Q1 2026 delivery) from unnamed "largest US semiconductor company." Quantum computing market early-stage; customer quality unknown.
- **HALEU:** TerraPower MOU + DFA (definitive framework agreement) supplies 2028-2037 at up to 150 MT/year. No advance purchases; production facility (QLE/Necsa) still in R&D phase.
- **Other Isotopes (Molybdenum-100, Zinc-68, Ytterbium-176, Nickel-64, Lithium-6/7, Chlorine-37):** No announced contracts. Development stage.

**Demand-Supply Gap:** Silicon-28 addressable market undefined (quantum computing TAM opaque, prices volatile). HALEU market addressable but contingent on TerraPower and similar advanced reactor deployment acceleration (politically dependent).

### 3. Macro & Policy Driver Analysis

**Policy Tailwinds:**
- **US Uranium Import Ban (Russia):** Effective 2024. Forces domestic substitution for HALEU and enriched uranium. Congress/DOE actively funding domestic capacity (DFC $500M Renergen co-finance signals government backing).
- **Inflation Reduction Act:** Bipartisan nuclear support; accelerates advanced reactor timelines. TerraPower beneficiary (Section 45Q tax credits, 90% cost-sharing available). ASPI indirect beneficiary via HALEU demand pull.
- **Advanced Reactor Deployment:** DOE estimates 2x-3x advanced reactor fleet by 2040 vs. base case. Each MW requires more HALEU than legacy enrichment levels.

**Policy Risks:**
- Advanced reactor timelines slip (funding delays, NRC licensing bottlenecks). TerraPower facility at Pelindaba contingent on South African regulatory approval and US repatriation agreements (unresolved).
- Silicon-28 demand dependent on quantum computing progress; no government mandate exists.

**Macro Environment:**
- Nuclear fuel prices: Spot uranium stable ~$65-70/lb (March 2026). Long-term contracts $60-65/lb. Specialty isotopes pricing opaque; no public market.
- Supply-side discipline from Kazatomprom, Orano, Centrus Energy. ASPI entry as 4th major player pressurizes margins if HALEU commoditizes (low risk near-term; medium risk by 2030+).
