# ASP Isotopes Inc. (ASPI)

**Date:** 03/03/2026
**Playbook:** uranium-and-nuclear-fuel
**Sector:** Uranium Enrichment & Isotope Production

## Executive Summary

ASP Isotopes is a pre-commercial stage advanced materials company developing proprietary isotope enrichment technologies — the Aerodynamic Separation Process (ASP) for lighter isotopes and Quantum Enrichment (QE) laser-based separation for heavier isotopes including uranium. The stock trades at ~$5.45 with a market cap of ~$687M. TTM revenue is $8.38M (up from $4.1M in 2024), driven by initial commercial isotope shipments (Silicon-28, Ytterbium-176, Carbon-12). The company reported a Q3 2025 net loss of $96.4M and holds $113.9M in cash following major equity raises. Through its UK subsidiary Quantum Leap Energy (QLE), ASPI is pursuing HALEU enrichment for advanced nuclear reactors — a market with only one licensed U.S. producer (Centrus Energy) and massive unmet demand. The investment thesis is high-risk/high-reward: proprietary enrichment technology with potential $50-70M revenue in 2026-2027 from isotope sales, plus long-term optionality on HALEU.

---

## Macro Context

As of March 2026, the nuclear fuel cycle is facing critical enrichment capacity constraints. Russia's Rosatom controls ~46% of global separative work unit (SWU) capacity, and the U.S. ban on Russian LEU imports (effective 2028 with waivers) is creating urgent demand for Western enrichment alternatives. Only Centrus Energy holds a U.S. NRC license for HALEU production, and its demonstration cascade produces limited quantities. Advanced reactor designs from Kairos, X-energy, TerraPower, and Oklo all require HALEU fuel (5-20% enriched uranium), but the supply chain barely exists outside Russia. The UK and U.S. governments are actively funding enrichment capacity expansion, with QLE entering early engagement with UK nuclear regulators. Meanwhile, stable isotope markets (Si-28 for quantum computing, C-14 for medical, Yb-176 for sensors) are experiencing growing demand from deep tech applications.

## Research

### 1. Enrichment Technology & Fuel Cycle Position

ASP Isotopes operates two proprietary enrichment platforms. The Aerodynamic Separation Process (ASP) is a gas-phase technique that separates isotopes using aerodynamic forces in high-speed gas flows — it is best suited for lighter isotopes such as Carbon-14, Silicon-28, Molybdenum-100, and certain noble gases. The Quantum Enrichment (QE) platform is a laser-based process that selectively excites and separates specific isotopes — it targets heavier isotopes including Ytterbium-176, Nickel-64, Lithium-6/Lithium-7, and ultimately Uranium-235. The company has successfully brought three enrichment facilities into operation in South Africa and has shipped first samples of Yb-176 and Si-28. For nuclear fuel applications, the QE platform is the critical pathway: if it can be scaled to enrich U-235 to HALEU levels (5-20%), it would provide a non-centrifuge alternative to the Russian-dominated enrichment supply chain. The HALEU facility timeline targets operational status in 2027, subject to regulatory approvals. ([Source: ASP Isotopes IR](https://ir.aspisotopes.com/))

### 2. Commercial Isotope Revenue Ramp

The near-term revenue story centers on stable isotope sales, not nuclear fuel. Silicon-28 (used in quantum computing research — higher isotopic purity enables longer qubit coherence times), Ytterbium-176 (used in atomic clocks, quantum sensors, and medical imaging), Carbon-12 (used in semiconductor and materials science), and Carbon-14 (medical diagnostics, carbon dating) represent the initial product portfolio. The company has guided to potential revenue of $50-70M during 2026-2027 from these isotope shipments, a substantial increase from $8.38M TTM. The key question is whether demand and pricing for enriched stable isotopes can sustain these revenue levels or whether initial shipments represent backlog fulfillment that decelerates. The isotope enrichment market is niche but growing, driven by quantum computing R&D and medical isotope demand. ([Source: ASP Isotopes Q3 2025](https://www.stocktitan.net/sec-filings/ASPI/10-q-asp-isotopes-inc-quarterly-earnings-report-b8eb89c3dea3.html))

### 3. HALEU & Nuclear Fuel Opportunity

The long-term bull case rests on HALEU enrichment. Advanced reactor designs — including Kairos FHR, X-energy Xe-100, TerraPower Natrium, and Oklo Aurora — require HALEU fuel at 5-20% U-235 enrichment, well above the 3-5% LEU used in existing reactors. Currently, only Centrus Energy holds a U.S. NRC license for HALEU production at its Piketon, Ohio demonstration cascade, producing limited quantities. Russia is the only other commercial-scale HALEU supplier. Through QLE, ASPI has entered early engagement with UK nuclear regulators for a proposed HALEU enrichment facility, with the UK Department for Energy Security confirming eligibility after national security checks. QLE is working with the Office for Nuclear Regulation on site selection, licensing, and permitting. If successful, this would make ASPI one of the first Western companies to offer a non-centrifuge HALEU production pathway. The company also targets Lithium-6 (used in tritium production for fusion and defense applications) with plans for a production plant operational in 2026. ([Source: TS2 Tech](https://ts2.tech/en/asp-isotopes-nasdaq-aspi-stock-ceo-comeback-haleu-progress-and-2026-outlook/))

### 4. Financial Position & Capital Structure

ASPI's financial profile reflects a pre-commercial deep tech company: high cash burn, repeated equity raises, and a market cap far exceeding current revenue. Q3 2025 showed $7.2M revenue against a $96.4M net loss, with $113.9M cash on hand. The company has funded operations primarily through equity issuances, and further dilution is likely if the isotope revenue ramp underperforms. At ~$687M market cap on $8.38M TTM revenue, the stock trades at ~82x revenue — pricing in both the isotope commercial ramp and significant HALEU optionality. The valuation is only justifiable if the $50-70M 2026-2027 revenue target materializes and HALEU regulatory progress de-risks the nuclear fuel thesis. Investors should size positions accordingly: this is a speculative, high-volatility name with potential for both multi-bagger returns and significant drawdowns. ([Source: Simply Wall St](https://simplywall.st/stocks/us/materials/nasdaq-aspi/asp-isotopes))
