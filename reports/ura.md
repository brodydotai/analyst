## Executive Summary

URA is the Global X Uranium ETF, tracking the Solactive Global Uranium & Nuclear Components Total Return Index with 51 holdings spanning uranium miners, enrichment companies, nuclear component manufacturers, and physical uranium trusts. The ETF trades at ~$56.45 with AUM of $6.36B, a 0.69% expense ratio, and a 4.15% dividend yield. Top holdings are heavily concentrated: Cameco (24.5%), Oklo (8.0%), Uranium Energy Corp (6.5%), NexGen Energy (5.6%), Kazatomprom (4.6%), Energy Fuels (4.0%), and Centrus Energy (3.7%) — the top 10 represent 69% of assets. The 52-week range of $19.50-$62.28 reflects the explosive rally in uranium equities driven by the structural supply deficit, AI data center nuclear demand, and the Russian LEU import ban. URA is the most liquid U.S.-listed vehicle for broad uranium sector exposure, though the concentrated top-heavy structure means performance is heavily driven by Cameco and a handful of names.

## Macro Context

As of March 2026, the uranium market is in the tightest supply-demand configuration since the 2007 supercycle. Uranium spot at ~$92/lb (down from $101.50 in January 2026) remains well above the $50-60/lb incentive price for new mine production. The structural deficit is widening: ~440 operating reactors consume ~180M lbs U3O8/year, while primary mine production covers only ~140M lbs. Secondary supply sources are depleting. The demand outlook is unprecedented: 60+ reactors under construction (heavily China/India), 20+ countries pledged to triple nuclear capacity by 2050 (COP28), AI hyperscaler nuclear PPAs (Microsoft-Constellation, Amazon-Talen, Google-Kairos) adding long-duration demand, and the Trump administration's Project Vault strategic uranium reserve. The Russian LEU import ban (effective 2028) forces Western fuel cycle reshoring, benefiting the entire URA portfolio. The macro risk is a broad commodity sell-off or nuclear policy reversal, but the structural demand drivers appear durable over a multi-year horizon.

3. Several URA holdings are pre-revenue or early-stage (Oklo at 8.0%, NexGen at 5.6%) where the stocks trade on narrative and optionality rather than cash flows — any execution failure or regulatory delay at these names amplifies downside in the ETF.

## Research

### 1. Portfolio Construction & Concentration

URA provides the broadest publicly available exposure to the uranium and nuclear fuel value chain. The fund holds 51 names spanning senior uranium producers (Cameco 24.5% — the world's largest publicly traded uranium company), junior miners (Uranium Energy Corp 6.5%, Denison Mines, Paladin Energy), enrichment companies (Centrus Energy 3.7%), advanced reactor developers (Oklo 8.0%), and physical uranium trusts. However, concentration is significant: the top 10 holdings represent 69% of assets, and Cameco alone is nearly a quarter of the fund. This means URA's performance is heavily correlated with Cameco's quarterly results, production guidance, and contract pricing. The inclusion of Oklo (8.0%) — a pre-revenue advanced reactor company — adds speculative growth exposure but also volatility. The 0.69% expense ratio is at the higher end for thematic ETFs. Compared to the more concentrated URNM (Sprott Uranium Miners ETF), URA offers slightly broader diversification including nuclear component makers. ([Source: ETF.com URA](https://etfdb.com/etf/URA/))

### 2. Supply/Demand Fundamentals

The global uranium market is in structural deficit. Approximately 440 reactors consume ~180M lbs U3O8 annually against ~140M lbs of primary mine production. Secondary supply — government stockpile drawdowns, enricher underfeeding, MOX recycling — has historically filled the gap but is depleting. Utility inventories are declining from historical levels of 24-36 months of forward coverage. The uncovered requirements ratio is rising, forcing utilities into a new contracting cycle that drives term price escalation. On the supply side, Kazatomprom (world's largest producer, ~43% of global output) has guided to flat-to-lower production, Cameco is ramping McArthur River/Key Lake but faces geological and operational challenges, and greenfield mine development requires 10-15 years. The uranium spot price of ~$92/lb is above the incentive price for ISL restarts (~$50-60/lb) but below the incentive price needed for greenfield conventional mines (~$80-100/lb), creating a Goldilocks zone that supports existing producers while discouraging adequate new supply. ([Source: Sprott Uranium Outlook](https://sprott.com/insights/uranium-outlook-2026/))

### 3. Demand Catalysts — AI, SMRs, and Policy

The uranium demand outlook has three distinct catalysts compounding simultaneously. First, AI data center power demand is driving hyperscaler nuclear PPAs — Microsoft's agreement with Constellation to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1, Amazon's deal with Talen Energy, and Google's partnership with Kairos Power each commit decades of baseload power demand. Second, SMR and advanced reactor deployment is accelerating — NuScale, GE-Hitachi BWRX-300, Rolls-Royce, and several HALEU-fueled designs are progressing through regulatory approval. Third, government policy is aggressively pro-nuclear: the U.S. ADVANCE Act, Project Vault strategic reserve, DOE enrichment contracts ($2.7B+), EU nuclear taxonomy inclusion, Japan reactor restarts, South Korea policy reversal, and the COP28 pledge to triple nuclear capacity by 2050. These three catalysts — AI power demand, SMR deployment, and government policy — are mutually reinforcing and create demand visibility extending decades. URA captures this entire thesis through its diversified exposure across the nuclear value chain. ([Source: Nasdaq Uranium Outlook](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/uranium-price-forecast-top-trends-uranium-2026))

### 4. Fund Mechanics & Comparison

URA is the most liquid U.S.-listed uranium ETF with $6.36B AUM, providing adequate institutional-grade liquidity. The 4.15% dividend yield (primarily from Cameco's dividends flowing through) adds income while waiting for capital appreciation. The 52-week return from $19.50 to $56.45 represents a ~190% gain, reflecting the market's recognition of the nuclear renaissance thesis. For investors seeking purer uranium price exposure, the Sprott Physical Uranium Trust (SRUUF) holds physical U3O8 directly. For more concentrated miner exposure, URNM (Sprott Uranium Miners) offers a different weighting methodology. URA's advantage is breadth — by including enrichers, component makers, and reactor developers alongside miners, it captures multiple vectors of nuclear value creation. The disadvantage is the 0.69% expense ratio and Cameco's dominant 24.5% weight, which makes URA function more like a "Cameco plus" product than a truly diversified sector ETF. ([Source: Morningstar URA](https://www.morningstar.com/etfs/arcx/ura/quote))
