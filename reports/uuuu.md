# Energy Fuels Inc. (UUUU)

**Date:** 03/03/2026
**Playbook:** uranium-and-nuclear-fuel
**Sector:** Uranium & Nuclear Fuel

---

## Executive Summary

Energy Fuels is the largest U.S. producer of natural uranium concentrate (U3O8) and the only domestic company with a fully integrated uranium-to-rare-earth processing capability at its White Mesa Mill in Utah. The stock trades at ~$23.40 with a market cap of ~$5.5B. In 2025, the company mined over 1.6 million pounds of uranium (exceeding guidance by 11%), produced over 1 million pounds of finished U3O8, and began pilot-scale production of separated heavy rare earth oxides (Dy, Tb, Sm). Working capital stands at $927M, including $797M in marketable securities and $65M cash, bolstered by a $700M convertible notes offering. Revenue remains modest relative to market cap — 2025 saw a net loss of $86.1M ($0.38/share) — but the company is positioned as a strategic asset play on U.S. critical minerals policy rather than a near-term earnings story.

## Macro Context

As of March 2026, uranium spot prices have pulled back to ~$92/lb from a January 2026 high near $101.50/lb, but remain well above the $50-60/lb incentive price needed for ISL restarts. The structural supply deficit is accelerating — global mine production covers roughly 75% of reactor demand, with secondary supply sources (government stockpiles, enricher underfeeding) steadily depleting. The Trump administration's "Project Vault" strategic uranium reserve initiative and $2.7B in new enrichment contracts signal durable U.S. policy support. Meanwhile, AI-driven data center buildouts are catalyzing new nuclear PPAs (Microsoft-Constellation, Amazon-Talen), adding long-duration demand visibility. The Russian LEU import ban (effective 2028 with waivers) is forcing Western fuel cycle reshoring, directly benefiting domestic producers like Energy Fuels.

3. Rare earth processing remains pre-commercial and depends on the Madagascar (Vara Mada) and Australian (Dubbo/ASM) feedstock pipelines materializing — any permitting or geopolitical disruption to these offshore sources would delay the REE thesis by years.

## Research

### 1. Fuel Cycle Position & Production Assets

Energy Fuels controls the White Mesa Mill — the only conventional uranium mill operating in the U.S. — and two active mining operations: the Pinyon Plain Mine (Arizona), which hosts the highest-grade uranium deposit in the country, and the La Sal Complex (Utah). In 2025, these assets mined over 1.6M lbs of recoverable uranium in ore, exceeding the top end of guidance by approximately 11%. The mill processed over 1M lbs of finished U3O8, with milling rates averaging ~250,000 lbs/month expected to continue through H1 2026. The company's 2026 guidance calls for 2.0-2.5M lbs of mined uranium, representing a 25-56% increase over 2025 levels. Energy Fuels also holds permits and standby capacity at several additional mines (Nichols Ranch ISR, Whirlwind, Sheep Mountain), providing production optionality as uranium prices support restart economics. ([Source: Energy Fuels 2025 Results](https://investors.energyfuels.com/2026-02-26-Energy-Fuels-Announces-2025-Results-and-2026-Guidance))

### 2. Rare Earth Element Strategy

The REE business represents Energy Fuels' most differentiated growth vector. The White Mesa Mill's Phase 1 separation circuit is already producing NdPr oxide and has begun pilot-scale production of heavy REE oxides (dysprosium, terbium, samarium). In December 2025, the company announced that its 99.9% purity dysprosium oxide passed quality assurance testing by major South Korean permanent magnet manufacturers — a critical commercial qualification milestone. The Phase 2 expansion feasibility study (released January 2026) projects a combined NPV of $3.7B across the White Mesa expansion and the Vara Mada project in Madagascar, with expected EBITDA of $765M for the first 15 years at lower-than-expected capex. Commercial-scale heavy REE production is targeted for Q4 2026. The pending ASM (Base Resources) acquisition would add the Dubbo project in NSW, Australia, as an additional feedstock source. ([Source: Energy Fuels REE Updates](https://investors.energyfuels.com/2025-07-17-Energy-Fuels-Now-Producing-Heavy-Rare-Earth-Element-Oxides))

### 3. Supply/Demand Dynamics & Uranium Market

The global uranium market remains in structural deficit. Approximately 440 operating reactors consume ~180M lbs U3O8 annually, while primary mine production covers only ~140M lbs. Secondary supply sources (stockpile drawdowns, enricher underfeeding, MOX recycling) are depleting, with the uncovered requirements ratio rising as utilities enter a new contracting cycle. Spot uranium peaked at $101.50/lb in January 2026 before pulling back to ~$92/lb. Long-term contract prices remain elevated at $75-80/lb, reflecting utility urgency. The supply deficit is expected to widen as ~60 reactors are under construction globally (heavily weighted toward China and India) and AI/data center demand drives incremental nuclear PPAs. The U.S. ban on Russian LEU imports (effective 2028) will further constrain enriched fuel supply, benefiting Western producers. ([Source: Sprott Uranium Outlook](https://sprott.com/insights/uranium-outlook-2026/))

### 4. Financial Position & Capital Structure

Energy Fuels ended 2025 with $927.4M in working capital — one of the strongest balance sheets in the uranium sector. This includes $64.7M cash, $797.1M in marketable securities (primarily equity holdings in other uranium/minerals companies), and the proceeds from a $700M 0.75% convertible notes offering. The net loss of $86.1M ($0.38/share) reflects the company's pre-revenue-ramp stage: uranium sales are constrained by contracted volumes while production costs are front-loaded during mine ramp-up. The company expects to sell 780K-880K lbs of U3O8 into long-term contracts in 2026, with potential for additional spot sales. If 850K lbs are sold at an average realized price of $70/lb, that implies ~$60M in uranium revenue alone — still well below market cap but representing meaningful sequential growth. Goldman Sachs initiated coverage with a Buy rating and $30 price target. The median analyst target of $26.88 across 6 analysts reflects a Strong Buy consensus. ([Source: Energy Fuels 2025 Results](https://investors.energyfuels.com/2026-02-26-Energy-Fuels-Announces-2025-Results-and-2026-Guidance))
