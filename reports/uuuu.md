## Research

### 1. Fuel Cycle Classification & Strategic Importance

Energy Fuels occupies a unique position in the US nuclear fuel cycle as the owner and operator of the only conventional uranium mill in the United States — the White Mesa Mill in Blanding, Utah. Licensed for over 8 million pounds of U3O8 per year, this facility processes uranium ore from multiple mine sources and has been adapted to also separate rare earth elements, making it a dual-purpose critical minerals asset.

The company's uranium assets span the full front-end fuel cycle at the mine and mill stages. Active operations include the Pinyon Plain Mine in Arizona (high-grade breccia pipe deposit, mining commenced December 2023), the White Mesa Mill (processing hub), and the Nichols Ranch ISR Project in Wyoming (2M lbs/yr capacity, currently on standby). The La Sal Complex, Whirlwind Mine, and Rim Mine provide additional permitted standby capacity for uranium and vanadium.

UUUU is also the only primary vanadium producer in the US, with 905,000 lbs of finished V2O5 in inventory as of September 2025. This positions the company across three critical mineral categories: uranium, rare earths, and vanadium.

### 2. Supply/Demand Fundamentals

**Production:** UUUU produced over 1 million pounds of finished U3O8 in 2025, with 2026 guidance calling for 2.0-2.5M lbs mined and 1.5-2.5M lbs processed. Sales guidance targets 780,000-880,000 lbs into long-term contracts plus spot/term opportunities. Milling rate is approximately 250,000 lbs/month through H1 2026.

**Costs:** Mining and transportation costs range $10-14/lb, milling adds $13-16/lb, yielding weighted average COGS of $23-30/lb — among the world's lowest for primary uranium production. Finished inventory cost averaged $53/lb as of September 2025.

**Market dynamics:** Global uranium spot prices reached $83-85/lb in January 2026, with US futures touching $100/lb (highest since February 2024). Long-term contract prices trade at $75-85/lb with the forward curve pricing above spot. Annual reactor demand stands at approximately 180M lbs vs. primary mine production of ~130M lbs, creating a structural deficit of roughly 28% that secondary supply (utility inventories, underfeeding, downblending) has been bridging but cannot sustain indefinitely.

**Contracting cycle:** Utility contracting volumes improved through 2025 with term market pricing incrementally higher, signaling belief in sustained demand. The supply deficit is forecast to persist and deepen through 2030 as reactor restarts and new builds (SMRs, AI data center demand) add incremental demand.

### 3. Macro & Policy Driver Analysis

The nuclear policy environment has shifted dramatically in UUUU's favor across multiple vectors.

**DOE commitment:** The US Department of Energy announced a $2.7 billion initiative in January 2026 for domestic uranium enrichment services over the next decade, explicitly supporting HALEU-related supply chain development. This signals long-term federal commitment to domestic nuclear fuel security.

**AI data center demand:** Meta's January 2026 agreement for up to 6.6GW of nuclear power through TerraPower's Natrium reactors (690 MW in two initial units by 2032-2035, plus rights to six additional units totaling ~2.1GW by 2035) established nuclear as essential AI infrastructure. This is not speculative — hyperscaler procurement of nuclear capacity validates the "nuclear for baseload AI" thesis.

**SMR pipeline:** Nine of ten government-funded advanced reactor designs require HALEU fuel. The DOE mandates at least 21 metric tons available to advanced reactor developers by June 2026, with demand projections of 50 MT/year by 2035 and 500 MT/year by 2050.

**Critical minerals designation:** Both uranium and rare earths carry critical/strategic mineral designations under current US policy, providing regulatory and procurement tailwinds. Executive orders and bipartisan legislative support for domestic nuclear fuel production reinforce this positioning.

### 4. Financial Logic & Valuation

**Revenue:** FY2025 revenue was $65.9M (down 15.6% from $78.1M in 2024), driven by timing of uranium sales. Q4 2025 revenue was $27.1M with uranium sales of ~360,000 lbs at $74.93/lb average.

**Profitability:** Net loss of $86M in FY2025 (vs. $47M in 2024). Loss widening driven by increased SG&A, exploration/development spending, and one-time charges. EPS was -$0.38 (vs. -$0.28 in 2024). The company is not yet cash-flow positive.

**Balance sheet:** As of September 2025, working capital was $298.5M, cash and equivalents $94.0M, marketable securities $141.3M, and inventory $74.4M. In January 2026, UUUU completed a $700M convertible senior notes offering (due 2031), bringing pro-forma liquidity to approximately $1 billion. Total shareholder equity was $707.6M with zero traditional debt.

**Valuation:** P/B ratio of 7.1x compares to peer average of 4.4x, reflecting the rare earth optionality premium. P/E is negative (-45.25x). Market cap stands at $5.16B with enterprise value of approximately $4.97B. Analyst consensus is Strong Buy (6 Buy, 1 Hold) with median price target of $26.88 (range $15.50-$34.00). H.C. Wainwright raised its target to $27.25 on February 27, 2026; Goldman Sachs initiated with a Buy rating.

**Capital allocation:** The $700M convertible provides ample funding for Phase 2 rare earth expansion ($410M capex) with significant remaining liquidity buffer. The capped call structure on the convertible reduces dilution impact.

### 5. Pattern Matching & IDPs

**Spot vs. contract price divergence:** US uranium futures touching $100/lb while spot trades at $83-85 signals forward market tightness. This divergence historically precedes sustained spot price increases as utilities are forced to contract at higher prices.

**Uncovered requirements pattern:** With the structural deficit at 28% and secondary supply sources depleting, utilities face growing uncovered forward requirements. This is the classic setup for a uranium bull market acceleration — utilities cannot defer procurement indefinitely.

**Physical trust accumulation:** Sprott Physical Uranium Trust and similar vehicles continue accumulating physical pounds, removing supply from the spot market and amplifying price signals. This financial demand layer is additive to utility procurement.

**Rare earth de-risking sequence:** Phase 1 commercial NdPr production (June 2024) → heavy REE qualification for permanent magnets (December 2025) → Phase 1 expansion operational Q4 2026 → Phase 2 BFS positive (January 2026, $1.8B NPV). Each milestone de-risks the next, creating a compounding confidence trajectory.

**Insider selling context:** EVP Timothy Carstens sold 59,594 shares at $14.93 in December 2025. Stock has appreciated to $21.32 since. Pattern is consistent with tax planning rather than loss of conviction, but bears monitoring.
