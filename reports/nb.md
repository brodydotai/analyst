## Research

### 1. Asset Profile & Critical Minerals Classification

NioCorp Developments Ltd. operates the Elk Creek Project, a 227-acre principal property in Nebraska plus additional optioned acreage. The deposit is classified as a high-grade intrusive carbonatite, the rarest and most valuable carbonatite type globally. Three minerals comprise the primary economic drivers: niobium (highest-grade in North America), scandium (2nd largest indicated-or-better rare earth resource in US), and titanium (value-add). Niobium is designated a US critical mineral under USGS 2024 criteria; scandium is also designated critical. Both minerals lack viable domestic primary supply; current US niobium imports are 100% dependent on ferroniobium from Brazil (Araxá).

**Critical Minerals Positioning:** US Department of Defense explicitly funded $10M under Defense Production Act Title III to advance Elk Creek, signaling Pentagon interest in supply chain diversification. The project is strategically aligned with executive orders mandating critical minerals supply security. Domestic production eliminates tariff/logistical exposure vs. Brazilian imports.

**Resource Grade:** Elk Creek's niobium resource grades are materially higher than Araxá's open-pit carbonatite profile. Scandium concentration is globally significant; only a handful of deposits worldwide contain economically extractable scandium at similar grades.

### 2. Project Economics & Development Status

**Capital Requirement:** Estimated total project cost is $1.1 billion, of which mine portal construction capex is $44.6M (commenced Q1 2026). This is a mid-tier capex project in mining terms, typical for underground carbonatite mining.

**Timeline:** Mine portal construction started Q1 2026. First production is not modeled until 2028–2029 (industry standard 24–36 month ramp for underground mining, accounting for permitting, engineering, and ore body delineation). No revenue until production.

**Financing Structure:**
- EXIM Bank: $780M loan targeted, advanced to Independent Technical Review phase. Binding term sheet targeted Q2 2026. This is a non-recourse project loan structure common in mining, wherein debt is repaid from project cash flow, not corporate balance sheet.
- UK Export Finance: Preliminary non-binding Expression of Interest for up to $200M guarantee (enhances debt capacity).
- Pentagon DPA Title III: $10M (capital contribution, not loan).
- Equity raised 2025: $370M+, including Feb 2026 $100M offering at $5.00/share.

**Cash Position:** $307M as of December 31, 2025 (record), zero traditional debt. This provides runway through FY 2027 and into FY 2028 without additional financing, assuming $44.6M annual mine capex and $42.7M annual net operating loss (current run rate Q3 2025).

**De-risking via Offtake:** 75% of ferroniobium production is under definitive offtake agreements (buyer secured, off-take pricing formula in place). This removes demand risk for the primary product and anchors project financing. 12 tons/year scandium offtake secured. Additional offtake agreements expected by April 2026 (likely titanium, additional rare earth metals).

### 3. Market Dynamics & Demand Drivers

**Niobium Market:**
- Global market size 2024: $1.92B.
- Projected 2033: $2.9B (5.31% CAGR).
- Current primary supply: 95.85% from carbonatite deposits, 100% dominated by Brazil's Araxá mine (CBMM control). No secondary sources.
- End-use demand: High-strength, low-alloy (HSLA) steel (largest), stainless steel, superalloys, capacitors.
- Supply concentration risk: Single producer (CBMM) in single country (Brazil) creates geopolitical vulnerability.
- Elk Creek production would diversify supply ~10–15% of global demand (typical for new primary mines), supporting CAGR growth without price collapse.

**Scandium Market:**
- Current demand: ~5 tons/year (extremely thin, nascent market).
- Projected 5-year demand: 50 tons/year (10x growth).
- End uses: Aluminum-scandium alloys (aerospace, defense, EV platforms for weight reduction), electronic ceramics, pigments.
- Supply: Byproduct of titanium and rare earth mining (limited and fragmented). No dedicated primary mines. Current supply is rationed by availability, not price (i.e., supply-constrained, not price-constrained).
- Elk Creek's 12 tons/year offtake represents ~24% of projected 5-year demand (50 tons/year). This is a significant market share for an emerging supply source, positioning NB as a strategic supplier.

**Demand Drivers:**
- EV platform electrification drives demand for lightweight aluminum alloys (scandium improves strength-to-weight ratios).
- Hypersonic/advanced aerospace (military) mandates scandium-enhanced materials.
- HSLA steel demand tied to infrastructure, automotive, and energy transition capex.
- US pentagon push for domestic supply removes price sensitivity; willingness to pay premium for domestic source (security premium).

## Summary for Perspectives

NioCorp Developments Ltd. (NASDAQ: NB) is a pre-revenue critical minerals developer with strategic Pentagon backing and project financing support. The company is advancing the Elk Creek Project in Nebraska, which contains the highest-grade niobium resource in North America and the 2nd largest indicated-or-better rare earth resource in the US. The project is uniquely positioned at the intersection of two structural supply constraints: niobium (95.85% supplied by Brazil's Araxá, posing geopolitical concentration risk) and scandium (virtually no primary supply, with demand projected to surge 10x in the next 5 years as aluminum-scandium alloys are adopted in aerospace and EV platforms).

**Financing Architecture:** NioCorp has secured $307M in cash (record, Q4 2025) with zero traditional debt. The company is advanced to Independent Technical Review phase with EXIM Bank, targeting a $780M project loan with binding term sheet expected Q2 2026. UK Export Finance is considering a $200M guarantee. Pentagon provided $10M under Defense Production Act Title III, explicitly funding supply chain diversification. This financing structure is typical for strategically important mining projects and significantly de-risks the estimated $1.1B capex requirement.

**De-Risking via Offtake:** 75% of ferroniobium production is under definitive offtake agreements, removing demand risk for the primary product. The company has secured 12 tons/year scandium offtake, representing ~24% of projected 5-year global demand (50 tons/year). Additional offtake agreements (titanium, rare earth metals) are expected by April 2026. Offtake agreements are the strongest form of production de-risking in mining and anchor project financing.

**Market Fundamentals:** Niobium market is projected to grow from $1.92B (2024) to $2.9B by 2033 (5.31% CAGR), primarily driven by HSLA steel demand and aerospace/defense alloy applications. Scandium demand is at an inflection point: current demand is ~5 tons/year (nascent market), but aluminum-scandium alloys offer 10–15% weight reduction in aerospace structures and are mandated in next-gen military platforms. EV platforms are evaluating scandium-enhanced aluminum as weight reduction becomes critical for range. This is not speculative demand; it is OEM specification-driven.

**Valuation and Upside:** NB trades at $5.62 with a consensus analyst price target of $7.73 (37% upside). Price targets range from $5.00 to $15.00, reflecting two scenarios: downside if financing delays or production timelines slip, and upside if EXIM term sheet closes and full offtake ramp is de-risked. At the current $307M cash position and strong balance sheet, the equity is priced to undervalue the combination of Pentagon-mandated supply chain security, offtake de-risking, and geopolitical tailwinds. The consensus rating is Moderate Buy (3 Buy, 1 Sell).

**Key Risks:** The largest risk is shareholder dilution. Share count doubled 114% in 7.5 months (June 2025 to Dec 2025), and a February 2026 $100M offering added 20M shares. While the strong balance sheet provides runway through 2027–2028, future cost overruns or financing delays could trigger additional dilutive equity raises. Mine development is complex; the $1.1B capex is pre-engineering and subject to typical mining cost overrun risk (20–40% is common). Offtake pricing is likely formula-based and tied to spot niobium and scandium prices; commodity downturn would reduce project returns. Finally, EXIM Bank financing is not closed; a pullback or restructuring below $600M would require material additional equity, destroying current shareholder value.

**Bull Perspective:** The setup is compelling. NB has solved the two hardest problems in mining: securing long-term offtake agreements (demand) and securing project financing (capital). The Pentagon backing is geopolitical, not cyclical. Scandium supply scarcity is structural, not temporary. Niobium production concentration in Brazil is a NATO-identified vulnerability. A successful EXIM term sheet and offtake finalization through Q2–Q3 2026 would de-risk the equity substantially, justifying $8–10/share (42–78% upside). The company's balance sheet provides a strong foundation; if EXIM financing closes, equity holders benefit from a $780M non-dilutive capital infusion.

**Bear Perspective:** The equity is expensive for a pre-revenue company with $1.1B capex ahead. Dilution risk is acute; another 50M shares could be issued before mine production begins. Mine development timelines routinely slip; if Elk Creek construction faces a 12–24 month delay, cash burn accelerates and additional equity is raised at weaker valuations. Offtake pricing is tied to commodity markets; niobium prices are volatile. If aerospace/automotive demand weakens, commodity prices and offtake economics compress. The geopolitical tailwind is real, but it doesn't guarantee project profitability; bad engineering or execution could still destroy shareholder value.

**Synthesis:** NB is a high-conviction, high-risk-to-reward critical minerals equity. Thesis success depends on three variables: EXIM financing closes as expected (Q2 2026), offtake agreements finalize (April 2026), and mine capex tracking stays on schedule (quarterly monitoring needed). If these three conditions hold, equity upside to $8–10/share is justified. If any one fails (EXIM pullback, offtake repricing, capex overrun), equity downside to $3–4/share is plausible. The geopolitical and supply-chain security tailwinds are real and multi-year. The short-term catalysts (EXIM, offtake) are binary; success is highly probable given Pentagon backing and offtake signatures, but execution risk is material. Investors should size positions accordingly and monitor quarterly financing/capex updates closely.
