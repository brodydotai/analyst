**Sector:** Critical Minerals — Niobium, Scandium, Rare Earths

## Executive Summary

NioCorp Developments is a pre-production critical minerals company developing the Elk Creek Project in southeast Nebraska — the highest-grade niobium resource in North America, also configured to produce scandium, titanium, and select rare earth elements. The stock trades at ~$5.63 with a market cap of ~$704M. NioCorp raised over $370M in 2025 (including $360.8M equity and $6.8M from the Department of War) and ended the year with a record $307M cash balance. In February 2026, the company launched construction of the Elk Creek mine portal — a ~$44.6M effort — and closed an additional $100M public offering. Total capital raised in 2025-2026 is approximately $500M. The company is pursuing $780M in EXIM Bank debt financing and a final investment decision (FID), with full project cost estimated at ~$1.2B. NioCorp is a pre-revenue development-stage company with zero production revenue and significant execution risk, but it controls a strategically positioned U.S. critical minerals asset at a time of aggressive government reshoring policy.

---

## Macro Context

As of March 2026, critical minerals supply chain security is a top-tier U.S. policy priority. China dominates global niobium processing (Brazil's CBMM controls ~80% of niobium mine production, but China is the largest consumer and increasingly vertically integrated). Scandium supply is extremely concentrated, with most production from China and Russia. The U.S. Critical Minerals List includes niobium, scandium, and several rare earths that Elk Creek could produce. The Department of War's investment in NioCorp signals defense establishment interest in domestic critical mineral sources. The EXIM Bank review process and bipartisan support for mineral supply chain reshoring provide policy tailwinds. However, the macro risk is real: niobium prices are stable but not surging (unlike uranium or copper), and the project's economic viability depends on successful scale-up and long-term offtake agreements that have not yet been publicly disclosed.

## Research

### 1. Commodity Classification & Strategic Importance

Niobium is a critical alloying element used primarily in high-strength low-alloy (HSLA) steels for infrastructure, automotive, and aerospace applications. Adding small amounts of niobium (0.01-0.05%) to steel increases strength by up to 30% while reducing weight — essential for lightweighting in EVs, aircraft, and modern infrastructure. Global niobium supply is effectively a duopoly: Brazil's CBMM controls ~80% of mine production, with Canada's Magris (formerly IAMGOLD's Niobec) providing most of the remainder. This concentration creates supply chain vulnerability that the U.S. government has flagged by placing niobium on the Critical Minerals List. Scandium is even more concentrated — used in aluminum-scandium alloys for aerospace and defense, with limited production primarily from China and Russia. Elk Creek's ability to co-produce niobium, scandium, titanium, and rare earths from a single deposit in the U.S. heartland represents a strategic asset class that few projects globally can match. ([Source: NioCorp](https://www.niocorp.com/niocorp-issues-2025-year-in-review-report/))

### 2. Project Economics & Development Status

The Elk Creek Project's total estimated cost is approximately $1.2B. NioCorp has raised ~$500M in 2025-2026 equity offerings and government funding. The company launched mine portal construction in February 2026 — a $44.6M contract to create the main underground access, filed a Notice of Commencement with MSHA, and expects portal work to take approximately nine months. Over 7,000 assay samples from infill drilling were completed in 2025, further de-risking the resource model. The critical remaining milestone is the $780M EXIM Bank loan, currently in Independent Technical Review — the final step before potential approval. Management has stated that FID and full construction cannot proceed until EXIM financing is secured. The project is designed as an underground mine feeding a processing facility, with a mine life expected to exceed 30 years. NioCorp has not publicly disclosed detailed economics (NPV, IRR) for the current project configuration including rare earths, as the feasibility study is being updated. ([Source: NioCorp Elk Creek](https://www.niocorp.com/niocorp-to-accelerate-pre-construction-activities-at-its-elk-creek-critical-minerals-project/))

### 3. Supply Chain Geopolitics & Policy Tailwinds

NioCorp is positioned squarely in the U.S. critical minerals reshoring narrative. The Department of War invested $6.8M of a committed $10M, reflecting direct defense interest in domestic niobium/scandium supply. The EXIM Bank review represents the largest potential government-backed financing for a U.S. critical minerals project outside of the DOE Loan Programs Office. Bipartisan support for mineral supply chain diversification from China and Russia provides durable policy cover. However, NioCorp faces competition for government attention from larger, more advanced projects (e.g., MP Materials for rare earths, Piedmont Lithium, Albemarle). The company's advantage is that no other U.S. project offers niobium production — it would be the only domestic source. Scandium production would similarly be a U.S. first, addressing a gap in the defense industrial base for aluminum-scandium alloys used in military aerospace. ([Source: Stock Titan](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/NB/nio-corp-issues-2025-year-in-review-asp7e4ue9vhq.html))

### 4. Financial Position & Dilution Risk

NioCorp ended 2025 with a record $307M cash balance after raising $370M+ during the year. An additional $100M public offering closed in February 2026, bringing total 2025-2026 capital raised to approximately $500M. The company has zero revenue and is entirely funded by equity issuances and government grants. Shares outstanding have increased substantially through serial offerings, contributing to the stock's decline from a $12.58 52-week high to $5.63. The 52-week range of $1.59-$12.58 illustrates extreme volatility typical of pre-revenue development-stage miners. The $307M+ cash position provides approximately 2-3 years of runway at current burn rates (primarily pre-construction spending and G&A), but the $1.2B total project cost means that even with EXIM financing, additional equity or debt raises may be needed to reach production. Investors must weigh the strategic asset value against ongoing dilution and the binary risk of EXIM approval. ([Source: Yahoo Finance NB](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/NB/))
