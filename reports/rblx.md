# Roblox Corporation (RBLX)

**Date:** 03/03/2026
**Playbook:** gaming-and-interactive-entertainment
**Sector:** Gaming / Interactive Entertainment / UGC Platform

---

## Executive Summary

Roblox is the world's largest user-generated content (UGC) gaming platform, connecting 144M daily active users (Q4 2025) with millions of creator-built experiences across mobile, PC, console, and VR. The stock trades at ~$83.20 with a market cap of ~$51B. Q4 2025 was a breakout quarter: revenue of $1.4B (+43% YoY), bookings of $2.2B (+63%), DAUs up 69% crossing 100M average, and engagement hours of 35B (+88%). Monthly unique payers nearly doubled to 37M, and developer payouts reached $477M in Q4 alone (+70% YoY). Full-year 2026 guidance calls for revenue of $6.02-6.29B (+23-29%) and bookings of $8.28-8.55B (+22-26%). Despite massive top-line growth, Roblox remains GAAP unprofitable with no clear timeline to GAAP profitability, though free cash flow generation is improving. The platform's network effects — 144M DAU creating content for each other — make it the closest analogue to a "gaming metaverse" in public markets.

---

## Macro Context

As of March 2026, the interactive entertainment sector is benefiting from secular growth in digital engagement, particularly among Gen Z and Gen Alpha demographics where Roblox has dominant market share. Global gaming revenue exceeds $200B annually, with mobile and free-to-play models driving the majority of growth. The macro environment is mixed for discretionary spending: elevated interest rates constrain some consumer wallets, but Roblox's core demographic (under-18) shows remarkably inelastic engagement — these users choose Roblox over competing entertainment options regardless of macro conditions. The competitive landscape is intensifying (Fortnite's UEFN creator tools, Minecraft's continued expansion, emerging AI-generated content platforms), but Roblox's 144M DAU and developer ecosystem create a two-sided network effect that is difficult to replicate. APAC expansion is the key geographic catalyst: Japan bookings grew 160%, India 110%, and Indonesia over 700% in Q4, suggesting early innings of global penetration.

## Bull Case

1. Roblox's 144M DAU platform with 69% YoY user growth represents one of the most powerful network effects in consumer technology — more users attract more creators, which attract more users, creating a compounding flywheel that competitors have failed to replicate.
2. APAC bookings surging 96% (Japan +160%, India +110%, Indonesia +700%) signals early-stage international monetization of a platform that has barely penetrated the world's largest gaming markets — APAC alone could double the addressable revenue base over 3-5 years.
3. Monthly unique payers nearly doubled to 37M (26% conversion rate from 144M DAU), indicating significant headroom for monetization improvement — mature free-to-play platforms typically convert 5-10% of DAU at 3-5x higher ARPPU than Roblox's current levels.

## Bear Case

1. At ~$51B market cap on $5.1B 2025 revenue (~10x), Roblox is priced for continued hypergrowth, yet the company is forecast to remain GAAP unprofitable for 3+ years — the path from bookings growth to earnings remains unproven.
2. Developer economics are structurally expensive: $477M in Q4 DevEx payouts (+70% YoY) grows in lockstep with bookings, meaning the creator payout ratio acts as a floor on margin improvement — Roblox cannot cut developer compensation without risking the ecosystem.
3. Regulatory and safety risks are material: Roblox's under-18 user base attracts constant scrutiny on child safety, data privacy, and content moderation — any major adverse regulatory action or PR crisis could impair user growth and advertiser willingness.

## Research

### 1. Platform Economics & User Engagement

Roblox's engagement metrics are extraordinary by any standard. Q4 2025 DAUs of 144M (+69% YoY) make it one of the largest daily-use platforms globally, rivaling major social networks. Engagement hours of 35B in Q4 (+88%) imply an average of ~4 hours per DAU per day — an engagement level that most entertainment companies cannot achieve. The DAU/MAU ratio (estimated ~50-55%) indicates a highly engaged user base. The platform operates as a two-sided marketplace: users create and consume experiences, with Roblox providing the engine, distribution, and monetization infrastructure. This UGC model gives Roblox content scale that no traditional game publisher can match — millions of experiences are available, with the long tail creating something for every niche interest. The economics are favorable: Roblox bears minimal content development cost (creators build the content), while collecting ~75% of gross bookings after developer payouts (~25% DevEx rate). The challenge is that developer compensation must remain competitive to retain top creators. ([Source: Roblox Q4 2025](https://ir.roblox.com/news/news-details/2026/Roblox-Reports-Fourth-Quarter-and-Full-Year-2025-Financial-Results/default.aspx))

### 2. Monetization & Bookings Growth

Q4 2025 bookings of $2.2B (+63%) and full-year guidance of $8.28-8.55B for 2026 (+22-26%) demonstrate strong monetization momentum. Monthly unique payers nearly doubled to 37M, representing a ~26% conversion rate from DAU — a metric that has significant expansion potential. Roblox monetizes primarily through Robux (in-platform currency) purchased for avatar cosmetics, experience access, and developer-created virtual items. The introduction of 18+ verified experiences is expanding the platform's addressable market beyond its traditional under-18 core, potentially opening higher ARPPU demographics. APAC bookings growth is the standout: U.S./Canada grew 41% (mature market), while APAC surged 96% — Japan (+160%), India (+110%), and Indonesia (+700%) represent nascent markets where Roblox has achieved product-market fit but barely begun monetizing. The 2026 guidance of 22-26% bookings growth explicitly excludes assumptions of a viral hit, providing conservative baseline expectations. ([Source: Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4548329-roblox-outlines-22-26-percent-bookings-growth-for-2026-while-expanding-ai-and-18-plus-market))

### 3. Creator Economy & Content Pipeline

Roblox's creator economy is its fundamental competitive moat. Q4 developer payouts of $477M (+70% YoY) represent the largest creator economy payout in gaming by a wide margin. Millions of developers build experiences on the platform, from solo hobbyists to professional studios, creating a content library that no traditional publisher can match in breadth. The DevEx program (Developer Exchange) pays creators ~25% of Robux spent in their experiences, with top creators earning millions annually. Roblox has invested heavily in AI-powered creation tools to lower the barrier to content development — generative AI for 3D assets, scripting assistance, and environment building — which could dramatically expand the creator base. The risk is competitive: Epic Games' Unreal Editor for Fortnite (UEFN) offers similar UGC capabilities with potentially higher developer revenue shares, and Minecraft continues to evolve its creator tools. However, Roblox's 144M DAU distribution advantage means creators go where the audience is, and no competitor offers comparable reach. ([Source: Roblox Earnings Call](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/05/roblox-rblx-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

### 4. Valuation & Path to Profitability

Roblox trades at ~$51B market cap on guided 2026 revenue of $6.02-6.29B (~8.2x revenue) and bookings of $8.28-8.55B (~6.0x bookings). The 22-analyst consensus is Buy with a median price target of ~$111 (range $67-$167). The valuation is demanding by traditional gaming multiples (EA trades at ~4x revenue, TTWO at ~5x) but reflects Roblox's platform economics and growth rate. The key valuation debate is profitability: Roblox remains GAAP unprofitable with no clear timeline to sustained GAAP net income. However, free cash flow is improving as the platform scales — higher bookings on a relatively fixed infrastructure cost base should eventually drive FCF margin expansion. Management has guided to ~26% FCF growth in 2026 despite higher capex for data centers. The structural challenge is that developer payouts, infrastructure costs, and trust & safety spending all scale with users, limiting the operating leverage that pure-SaaS models enjoy. Investors are effectively paying for the option that Roblox becomes the dominant social-gaming platform globally and eventually converts its massive audience into sustainable profitability. ([Source: TipRanks](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/rblx/forecast))
