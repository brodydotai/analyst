# Centrus Energy Corp. (LEU)

**Date:** 03/03/2026

**Sector:** Uranium Enrichment & Nuclear Fuel Services

## Executive Summary

Centrus Energy is the only U.S. company licensed by the NRC to produce HALEU (High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium) and the leading Western intermediary for nuclear fuel services, with a $3.8B total order backlog extending to 2040. The stock trades at ~$196.50 with a market cap of ~$3.1B. Full-year 2025 revenue was $448.7M (+1.5% YoY), net income was $77.8M ($3.90/diluted share), and gross profit rose 5% to $117.5M. SWU revenue grew 21% (on 23% volume increase) while uranium revenue declined 54% due to delivery timing. The company delivered 900 kg of HALEU UF6 to the DOE under its Piketon demonstration cascade and produced over one metric ton by year-end. Centrus has been selected for a $900M DOE HALEU task order (subject to negotiation) and guided 2026 revenue to $425-475M. With a $2.9B LEU segment backlog and sole-source positioning for U.S. HALEU production, Centrus is the critical chokepoint in America's advanced nuclear fuel supply chain.

---

## Macro Context

As of March 2026, the nuclear fuel enrichment landscape is defined by a single structural reality: Russia's Rosatom controls ~46% of global SWU (separative work unit) capacity, and the U.S. ban on Russian LEU imports becomes binding in 2028. Western enrichment capacity (URENCO, Orano) is fully contracted, leaving minimal spot availability. Advanced reactors (Kairos, TerraPower, X-energy, Oklo) all require HALEU fuel at 5-20% enrichment, but only Centrus holds an NRC license to produce it domestically — at a demonstration cascade producing ~900 kg/year. The DOE is actively funding enrichment expansion ($2.7B in new contracts), and the NNSA has signaled intent to sole-source certain enrichment activities to Centrus. Uranium spot at ~$92/lb and rising SWU prices ($180+/SWU, up from $130 two years ago) reflect tightening fuel cycle economics that directly benefit Centrus's intermediary and production businesses.

## Bull Case

1. Centrus is the sole NRC-licensed U.S. HALEU producer with a $900M DOE task order in negotiation — in a market where every advanced reactor design requires HALEU and no other domestic source exists, this creates an effective monopoly position.
2. The $3.8B total backlog (extending to 2040) with $2.9B in firm LEU commitments provides exceptional revenue visibility, while rising SWU prices ($180+/SWU) expand margins on new contracts.
3. NNSA notification of intent to sole-source enrichment activities to Centrus opens potential classified defense applications (naval reactors, tritium production) — a high-margin, long-duration revenue stream that the market has not fully priced.

## Bear Case

1. At ~$3.1B market cap on $448.7M revenue (7x revenue), LEU is priced for significant HALEU scale-up that has not yet materialized — the Piketon cascade produces only 900 kg/year, far below the multi-ton quantities needed for commercial reactor fueling.
2. Centrus relies heavily on purchasing SWU from Rosatom/TENEX under existing contracts — the Russian import ban timeline (2028) creates transition risk if alternative Western SWU supply is insufficient to meet delivery obligations.
3. The $900M HALEU task order is "subject to negotiation" and funding remains contingent on Congressional appropriations — any budget dispute, continuing resolution, or policy shift could delay or reduce the award.

## Research

### 1. HALEU Production & Fuel Cycle Position

Centrus operates the only NRC-licensed HALEU production facility in the United States — the American Centrifuge Plant in Piketon, Ohio. Under Phase II of the DOE operations contract, Centrus delivered 900 kg of HALEU UF6 and produced over one metric ton by year-end 2025. The Phase III contract extension (valued at ~$110M through June 2026) continues production at a 900 kg/year rate. HALEU is uranium enriched to 5-20% U-235, required by virtually every advanced reactor design: Kairos Power's fluoride salt-cooled high-temperature reactor, X-energy's Xe-100 pebble bed, TerraPower's Natrium, and Oklo's Aurora. The DOE's selection of Centrus for a $900M HALEU task order — the largest single enrichment award in decades — signals federal commitment to scaling domestic HALEU capacity. Centrus's proprietary American Centrifuge technology, developed over $5B+ in prior government and private investment, represents a sunk-cost advantage that would take any competitor years and billions to replicate. ([Source: Centrus Q4 2025 Results](https://investors.centrusenergy.com/news-releases/news-release-details/centrus-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2025-results-and))

### 2. LEU Segment & Backlog Economics

The LEU segment — Centrus's core business — generated $346.2M in 2025 revenue, primarily from SWU and uranium deliveries under medium and long-term utility contracts. SWU revenue grew 21% on a 23% volume increase, reflecting rising contract activity as utilities rebuild forward coverage. The LEU backlog stands at $2.9B in firm commitments, with an additional $2.3B in contingent backlog, extending through 2040. This backlog provides multi-year revenue visibility: at $425-475M guided 2026 revenue, the firm backlog alone represents ~6-7 years of forward sales. Centrus operates as an intermediary — purchasing SWU from enrichers (including TENEX/Russia under existing contracts, and URENCO/Orano) and reselling to U.S. and international utilities at a margin. As SWU spot prices have risen from ~$130 to $180+/SWU over two years, margins on new contracts are expanding. The Russian supply chain transition is the key risk: Centrus must shift procurement from TENEX to Western sources before the 2028 import ban takes full effect. ([Source: Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4550608-centrus-projects-425m-475m-2026-revenue-as-900m-haleu-award-advances-u-s-enrichment-build-out))

### 3. Government Contracts & Defense Applications

Beyond commercial HALEU, Centrus is positioned for classified defense enrichment work. The NNSA notified Centrus of its intent to sole-source certain uranium enrichment activities — a signal that national security applications (naval reactor fuel, tritium production feedstock) may require Centrus's centrifuge technology. Defense enrichment contracts are typically classified, long-duration, cost-plus arrangements with superior margin profiles and minimal competitive pressure. The DOE's broader $2.7B enrichment expansion includes partnerships with Centrus and other entities to rebuild domestic fuel cycle capacity that atrophied after the closure of the Paducah gaseous diffusion plant. Centrus's Piketon facility retains the physical infrastructure and NRC licenses that would take a new entrant 5-10 years to obtain, creating a regulatory moat as significant as its technological one. The stock has rallied ~385% from its pre-HALEU lows, reflecting the market's recognition of this strategic positioning. ([Source: TipRanks](https://www.tipranks.com/news/company-announcements/centrus-energy-earnings-call-highlights-haleu-pivot))

### 4. Valuation & Financial Health

Centrus trades at ~$196.50 with a $3.1B market cap against $448.7M in 2025 revenue (~7x) and $77.8M net income (~40x P/E). The 14-analyst consensus rating is Buy with a median price target of $300 (range $125-$390), implying ~53% upside from current levels. The valuation reflects HALEU optionality: at current production rates, the Piketon cascade generates modest revenue, but full-scale enrichment buildout (funded by the $900M task order) could add hundreds of millions in recurring revenue at government-contract margins. Balance sheet health has improved — Centrus has manageable debt levels relative to its cash flow generation and the $3.8B backlog provides collateral for any future financing needs. The key financial risk is lumpy revenue recognition: SWU deliveries are contractually scheduled, creating quarter-to-quarter volatility (uranium revenue dropped 54% in 2025 on timing). Investors should evaluate LEU on an annual basis, not quarterly, given delivery cadence dynamics. ([Source: MarketBeat](https://www.marketbeat.com/stocks/NYSE/LEU/forecast/))
