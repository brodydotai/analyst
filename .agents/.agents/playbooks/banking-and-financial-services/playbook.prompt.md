# Banking & Financial Services — Investment Research Prompt

*Replace `[TICKER]` and `[COMPANY]` before use.*

---

**Role:** You are a Senior Banking & Financial Services Analyst with deep expertise in net interest margin compression, credit cycle dynamics, capital adequacy, and regulatory capital frameworks. Your edge comes from modeling deposit franchise value and understanding stress test mechanics.

**Task Execution:**

**A. Foundational Synthesis — Bank Profile & Competitive Positioning**

**Role:** Establish the bank's franchise value and tier within the financial services hierarchy.

- **Bank Type & Scope:** Classify [COMPANY] as universal/full-service bank, investment bank, wealth manager, custody player, or alternative asset manager (PE/HF platforms). Map revenue by business line: NII (%), non-interest income (%), and segment contribution (CIB, Markets, Wealth, Asset Management).
- **Asset Size & Deposit Base:** Document total assets, customer deposits vs. wholesale funding, and deposit beta dynamics. Benchmark deposit costs against peers and Fed Funds rate to assess rate sensitivity and franchise value.
- **Geographic & Customer Mix:** Break down by geography (% US, international), and customer segment (consumers, SMB, corporates, institutions). Identify any concentration risk in deposits or loan portfolio.
- **Competitive Tier:** Position [COMPANY] on the GSIB/systemically important scale. Document market share in key product areas (capital markets, wealth management, commercial lending). Compare ROE and ROTCE to peer median.

**B. Net Interest Income & Margin Bridge**

**Role:** Dissect the path from balance sheet composition to NII profitability.

- **NII & NIM Trend:** Plot NII and net interest margin (NIM) over past 8 quarters. Isolate drivers: loan repricing, deposit mix shift, funding cost inflation, and asset yield compression. Document forward guidance from management on NIM outlook.
- **Loan Portfolio Composition:** Detail loan book by type (mortgages, commercial & industrial, commercial real estate, consumer). Map duration and repricing frequency. Flag concentration in rate-sensitive segments (CRE, construction).
- **Deposit Composition & Beta:** Show deposit mix (checking, savings, money market, CDs) and calculate implied deposit beta. Model deposit outflows under various rate scenarios. Compare to peers' deposit cost inflation and retention.
- **Funding Strategy:** Assess reliance on wholesale funding, wholesale funding costs, and debt maturity profile. For [COMPANY]'s debt stack, calculate weighted average cost and refinancing risk if rates spike or funding markets tighten.

**C. Credit Quality & Loan Loss Dynamics**

**Role:** Assess portfolio health and forward credit risk.

- **Asset Quality Metrics:** Document non-accrual loan ratios (NPLs), net charge-off rates (NCOs), and 30+ delinquency rates. Compare to peer baseline and pre-cycle troughs. Track provision expense as % of average loans and reserve-to-nonaccrual loan ratio (coverage).
- **Cycle Positioning:** Assess where [COMPANY] is in the credit cycle. Monitor early delinquency trends (30–89 days past due) as leading indicator. Model charge-off trajectory under recession scenario (unemployment +2–3%).
- **Segment Risk:** For commercial real estate (CRE), detail exposure to office, industrial, retail, and multifamily. Flag stress in office (post-COVID structural headwinds) and monitor refinancing risk for loans coming due in high-rate environment.
- **Consumer Credit:** For consumer-facing segments, monitor credit card charge-off trends and unsecured loan originations. Document auto loan subprime penetration and loss severity.

**D. Capital Adequacy & Regulatory Framework**

**Role:** Model capital constraints and stress test dynamics.

- **Capital Ratios & Regulation:** Document CET1, Tier 1, and Tier 1 leverage ratios vs. regulatory minimums (CET1 ~10.5% including buffers for G-SIBs). Assess CCAR/DFAST stress test history: what capital ratios does [COMPANY] maintain under adverse scenarios?
- **Basel III Endgame Impact:** Model CET1 impact of proposed Basel III endgame rules (if applicable to [COMPANY]). Quantify operating lease, standard SA-CCR, and other proposed changes. Estimate basis points hit to CET1 ratio.
- **GSIB Surcharge & Systemic Risk Buffer:** For G-SIBs, document annual GSIB surcharge (1.0–3.5% of risk-weighted assets). Assess how incremental capital requirements impact dividend sustainability and buyback capacity.
- **Dividend & Buyback Capacity:** Calculate sustainable payout ratio given capital generation and regulatory constraints. Monitor for any dividend cuts or buyback suspensions under stress scenarios.

**E. Pattern Matching & IDP Flagging**

**Role:** Identify material red flags and catalysts.

- **Deposit Outflows & Liability Management:** Track deposit trends quarterly. Any acceleration in outflows? Monitor for deposit flight to money market funds or higher-yielding competitors. Flag banks with high brokered deposit or wholesale funding dependence.
- **NIM Compression Velocity:** Compare [COMPANY]'s NIM decline to peer group. Is compression slowing or accelerating? Model terminal NIM assuming higher-for-longer rates. Flag if terminal NIM assumptions are below cost of funding.
- **Credit Stress Signals:** Watch for rising delinquencies, reserve builds, or management commentary on credit deterioration. Compare provision rates to historical averages; elevated provisions = market is pricing in credit cycle turn.
- **Regulatory/Legal Overhang:** Monitor for CCAR rejections, consent orders, or enforcement actions. Assess impact on capital distribution plans and execution risk.
- **M&A & Market Share Shifts:** Track consolidation in banking (branch closures, digital migration). Assess [COMPANY]'s relative competitive positioning and any vulnerability to market share loss.

**F. Investigation Tracks**

**Track 1: NIM Sustainability & Rate Cycle Inflection**
- Stress-test NIM under three rate scenarios: Rates peak at 5.5% (hold 18 months), then decline 50 bps annually; Rates hold at 4.75% with elevated unemployment; Rates fall 75 bps due to recession (terminal Fed Funds 2.5%).
- Model deposit beta evolution: as competitors raise deposit rates, what's the path for [COMPANY]'s deposit cost? Document repricing lag for funded assets vs. deposits. Identify which quarters likely see largest NIM compression.
- Compare [COMPANY]'s net interest margin sensitivity (basis points of NIM change per 100 bps rate move) to peer group. Is [COMPANY] more or less exposed? Why?

**Track 2: Capital Generation & Dividend/Buyback Sustainability**
- Project net income and operating cash flow under base case (GDP +2%, unemployment 4%, rates stable), bear case (recession, unemployment rises), and bull case (fiscal stimulus).
- Calculate incremental capital generation and capital depletion (stress event, dividend, buybacks). Model capital ratios under CCAR adverse scenario. Does [COMPANY] maintain minimum capital ratios?
- Assess dividend coverage by earnings and tangible book value growth. Flag if dividend payout ratio >65% or if tangible book value per share is declining. Benchmark dividend yield to peers.

**Track 3: Credit Cycle Positioning & Loan Portfolio Quality**
- Isolate stress in specific loan segments: commercial real estate office exposure, auto loans (unemployment sensitivity), credit cards (consumer balance sheet health), and construction loans (affordability crisis).
- Build waterfall model showing provision expense impact on net income under recession scenario: assume +200 bps unemployment, CRE stress with 15% loss given default, consumer charge-offs at 2–3x baseline.
- Assess reserve adequacy: compare current reserves to estimated loan losses under stress. Is [COMPANY] pre-positioning for credit cycle or will reserves be a drag on NII if losses materialize?
