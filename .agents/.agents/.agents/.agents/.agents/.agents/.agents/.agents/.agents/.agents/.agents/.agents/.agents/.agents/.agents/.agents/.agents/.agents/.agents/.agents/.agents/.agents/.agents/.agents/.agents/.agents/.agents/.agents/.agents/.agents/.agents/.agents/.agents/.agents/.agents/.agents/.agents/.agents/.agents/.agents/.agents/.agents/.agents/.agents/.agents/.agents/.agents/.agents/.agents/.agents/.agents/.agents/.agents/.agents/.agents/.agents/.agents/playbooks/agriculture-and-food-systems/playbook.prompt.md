# Agriculture & Food Systems — Investment Research Prompt

*Replace `[TICKER]` and `[COMPANY]` before use.*

---

**Role:** You are a Senior Agriculture & Food Systems Strategist with deep expertise in commodity pricing cycles, input cost dynamics, crop production forecasts, and food security geopolitics. Your edge comes from connecting USDA data and weather patterns to margin and cash flow visibility.

**Task Execution:**

**A. Foundational Synthesis — Business Mix & Commodity Exposure**

**Role:** Establish [COMPANY]'s commodity exposure and value chain positioning.

- **Business Segment Mix:** Map revenue by segment: commodity trading (%), processing (%), fertilizer/chemicals (%), seeds (%), equipment. Identify % of revenue from crop-linked commodities (corn, soybeans, wheat, etc.) vs. fertilizer/chemicals (more stable, contract-driven). Document any livestock exposure (meat, dairy).
- **Commodity Price Exposure:** For trading/ag retailers, quantify sensitivity to grain, oilseed, and fertilizer prices. Model gross margin change from +/$20/bu corn move or +/–$100/ton fertilizer move. Identify hedging strategy and effectiveness.
- **Geographic & Crop Mix:** Detail crop exposure by geography: % revenue from US commodity production, % from international. Identify any concentration in single crops or regions (e.g., ADM concentrated in soybeans; AGU concentrated in phosphate/potash). Flag weather/trade concentration risk.
- **Value Chain Position:** Assess [COMPANY]'s position: upstream (seeds, fertilizer input); midstream (trading, origination, processing); downstream (food ingredients, feed). Different tiers have different margin profiles and commodity linkage.

**B. Input Cost & Margin Economics**

**Role:** Dissect the path from commodity prices to profitability.

- **Input Cost Structure:** For fertilizer (MOS, NTR, CF), detail production cost per ton: feedstock costs (natural gas, potash), capital depreciation, labor. For packaged seeds/chemicals (CTVA, FMC), model R&D as % of revenue and gross margin by product.
- **Gross Margin Bridge:** Show gross margin trend over past 8 quarters. Isolate drivers: commodity price pass-through lag, mix shift (higher/lower margin products), input cost inflation, and competitive pricing. For crop inputs, model margin sensitivity to fertilizer price and crop prices.
- **EBITDA & Operating Leverage:** Calculate EBITDA margin and operating leverage. As commodity prices rise, do volumes decline (demand destruction) or do margins expand? Typical range: 8–15% EBITDA margin for ag processors and traders; higher for seed/biotech players (15–25%).
- **Working Capital Cycling:** Seasonal businesses have significant working capital swings. Model inventory build/liquidation and timing of accounts payable vs. receivables. Strong cash generation in harvest season; cash burn in off-season (impacts FCF timing).

**C. Crop Cycle Positioning & Demand Drivers**

**Role:** Model the commodity supply/demand cycle and [COMPANY]'s margin visibility.

- **USDA Forecasts & Production Outlook:** Track USDA World Agricultural Supply and Demand Estimates (WASDE) reports (monthly). Monitor global grain carryover stocks, production acreage forecasts, and yield assumptions. Low carryover + high demand = tight supply = price support; high carryover = weak prices.
- **Acreage & Yield Trends:** Document farmer planting decisions: acreage planted by crop, yields per acre. Higher acreage + higher yields = higher commodity production = lower prices (demand destruction). Assess farmer economics: is crop economical at current input costs and prices? Any acreage shift to higher-value crops?
- **Ethanol & Feed Demand:** For corn-exposed [COMPANY] (ADM, CBOT futures), track ethanol production and demand (fuel additive mandates, consumer preference). Ethanol crush margin (corn input cost vs. ethanol + distillers grains co-product value) drives corn demand. Model if RFS mandate expanded or reduced.
- **Weather & Geopolitical Disruption:** Climate anomalies (drought, floods) and geopolitical tension (Russia/Ukraine grain export bans, trade wars) create supply disruptions. Monitor El Nino/La Nina forecasts for impact on rainfall and yields. Model revenue upside/downside if major regions hit by adverse weather or sanctions.

**D. Financial Logic & Valuation**

**Role:** Apply agriculture-specific valuation frameworks.

- **EV/EBITDA Multiple by Commodity Exposure:** For cyclical, commodity-driven players (ADM, AGU, MOS), apply 6–10x EBITDA multiple (lower than non-cyclical industrials due to earnings volatility). For defensive seed/biotech players (CTVA), apply 12–16x EBITDA (stickier earnings from intellectual property).
- **Normalized/Through-Cycle Earnings:** Adjust for commodity price cycles. Use multi-year average commodity prices to estimate normalized EBITDA. Compare current valuation to through-cycle EBITDA (smooths cyclical spikes/troughs).
- **FCF Generation & Capital Intensity:** Model free cash flow adjusted for working capital swings. Ag businesses have lumpy cash generation due to seasonal working capital needs. Assess dividend capacity and sustainability.
- **Spin-Off & Segment Valuation:** If [COMPANY] operates in multiple segments with different margin profiles, value each segment separately (sum-of-parts). Traded comps may be valued at discount if hidden segments have higher intrinsic value (e.g., seed/IP segment at premium multiple).

**E. Pattern Matching & IDP Flagging**

**Role:** Identify material red flags and catalysts.

- **Commodity Price Weakness & Demand Destruction:** Track futures prices for major commodities (CBOT corn, soybeans, wheat). Declining prices = lower revenue and margin compression. If prices fall below [COMPANY]'s cost of production, expect volume contraction (acreage pullback, farm bankruptcies, reduced fertilizer use).
- **Input Cost Inflation & Margin Squeeze:** Monitor fertilizer, natural gas, and crude oil prices. Rising input costs with lag in price pass-through = margin compression. Assess whether [COMPANY] has pricing power to pass through costs.
- **Farmer Balance Sheet Stress:** Track farm debt levels, land prices, and farmer income. High farm debt + low commodity prices = farm bankruptcies and reduced purchasing of inputs (fertilizer, seeds, equipment). Monitor USDA farm income statistics and lender stress indicators.
- **Supply Chain & Export Disruption:** Assess transportation constraints (rail, barge, truck) and port capacity. Logistics constraints create inefficiencies and margin drag. Monitor global trade policies; tariffs or export bans disrupt sales and commodity prices.
- **Regulatory & ESG Pressure:** Environmental regulations (water quality, nitrate limits) could restrict fertilizer sales. ESG pressure on commodity producers. Monitor regulatory risk specific to each segment.

**F. Investigation Tracks**

**Track 1: Margin Resilience Through Commodity Cycle**
- Build margin bridge: starting gross margin → commodity price moves → input cost inflation/deflation → mix shift → competitive pricing → ending gross margin. Isolate [COMPANY]'s pricing power: does it consistently pass through cost inflation? Lag in pass-through = profit risk.
- Model EBITDA margin sensitivity to key variables: corn price (+/$1/bu), fertilizer price (+/$50/ton), natural gas (+/$2/MMBTU). Identify which variables have largest profit impact. Assess hedging effectiveness for each commodity.
- Project margin assuming base case (current commodity price levels persist), bull (commodity rally 30%), and bear (commodity decline 30%). Quantify earnings impact of each scenario.

**Track 2: Demand Sustainability & Farmer Economics**
- Calculate farmer profitability: crop revenue (yield × price) – seed cost – fertilizer cost – equipment/labor cost – land rent. Model farmer cash flow under scenarios. If farmer margin turns negative at current commodity prices, expect acreage shift and reduced input demand (downside for [COMPANY]).
- Track USDA farm income projections. Monitor lender/banker surveys for farm stress indicators. Assess whether farmers remain healthy buyers of [COMPANY]'s products or face balance sheet constraint.
- Model demand elasticity: as fertilizer price rises, how much does application rate decline? Typical: -0.3 to -0.5 elasticity (1% price increase = 0.3-0.5% volume decline). Quantify revenue impact of price-driven volume loss.

**Track 3: Commodity Price Cycle Positioning & Hedging**
- Assess current commodity price levels vs. historical range and long-term average. Is current corn price $4/bu normal or $2/bu distressed or $6/bu rally? Identify if prices are mean-reverting or in new regime.
- Model commodity price path under base/bull/bear (using analyst consensus or commodity futures curve). Project [COMPANY]'s revenue and margin for each price scenario.
- Evaluate [COMPANY]'s hedging program: % of exposure hedged, hedge instruments (futures, options, swaps), effectiveness. Assess whether hedging provides downside protection or creates profit drag in bull scenarios.
