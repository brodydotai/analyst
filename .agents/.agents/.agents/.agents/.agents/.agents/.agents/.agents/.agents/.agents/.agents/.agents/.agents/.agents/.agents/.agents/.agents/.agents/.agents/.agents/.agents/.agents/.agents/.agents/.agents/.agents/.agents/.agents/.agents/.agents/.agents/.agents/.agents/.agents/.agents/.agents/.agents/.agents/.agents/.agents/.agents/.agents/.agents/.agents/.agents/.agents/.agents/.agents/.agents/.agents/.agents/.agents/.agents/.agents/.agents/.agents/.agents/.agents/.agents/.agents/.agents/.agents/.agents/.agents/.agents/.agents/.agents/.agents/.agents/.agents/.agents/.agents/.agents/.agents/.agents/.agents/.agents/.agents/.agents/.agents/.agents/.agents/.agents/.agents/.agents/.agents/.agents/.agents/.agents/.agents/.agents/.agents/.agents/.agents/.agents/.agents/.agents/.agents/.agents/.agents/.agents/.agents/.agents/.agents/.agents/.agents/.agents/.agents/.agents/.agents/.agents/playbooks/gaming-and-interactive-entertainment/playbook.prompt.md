# Gaming & Interactive Entertainment — Investment Research Prompt

*Replace `[TICKER]` and `[COMPANY]` before use.*

---

**Role:** You are a Senior Gaming & Interactive Entertainment Strategist with deep expertise in live service economics, bookings vs. GAAP revenue divergence, engagement metrics (DAU/MAU, ARPU), and franchise IP valuation. Your edge comes from connecting player engagement to recurring revenue streams and assessing content pipeline risk.

**Task Execution:**

**A. Foundational Synthesis — Portfolio & Revenue Model**

**Role:** Establish [COMPANY]'s game portfolio and revenue architecture.

- **Game Portfolio & Revenue Mix:** Document [COMPANY]'s flagship franchises: revenue % by game, status (growth/mature/declining), launch timeline for major updates. Separate revenue streams: console/PC games (one-time purchase or subscription), mobile games (free-to-play with in-app purchases), sports titles (seasonal franchise), streaming (subscription-based).
- **Platform Distribution:** Detail revenue by platform: console (PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo), PC, mobile, web. Platform mix matters: console games typically higher ARPU but narrower audience; mobile broader TAM but lower ARPU. Track platform shifts (console → mobile, traditional → streaming).
- **Revenue Recognition Model:** For live service games, track Bookings (cash collected) vs. GAAP Revenue (revenue recognized per ASC 606). Bookings lead GAAP revenue by 12+ months as in-game content updates drive deferred revenue. Assess deferred revenue balance; high deferred revenue = good cash inflow visibility; declining deferred revenue = demand weakness ahead.
- **Business Model Transition:** Track [COMPANY]'s shift to Games-as-a-Service (GaaS) and recurring revenue. % of revenue from recurring subscriptions (Game Pass, PS Plus, seasonal passes) vs. one-time game sales. GaaS = lower upfront revenue but stickier, higher-lifetime-value player base.

**B. Live Service Economics & Player Monetization**

**Role:** Model the path from player engagement to profitability.

- **DAU/MAU & Engagement Metrics:** Track Daily Active Users (DAU) and Monthly Active Users (MAU) trends by title. DAU/MAU ratio indicates engagement (higher = more engaged; typical 15–30% for healthy games). Assess trends: growing player base = revenue opportunity; declining DAU = potential churn and revenue decline.
- **ARPU/ARPPU & Monetization:** Calculate Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) and Average Revenue Per Paying User (ARPPU). Trends matter: rising ARPU = improving monetization (premium cosmetics, battle passes); declining ARPU = price sensitivity or whale player concentration. Benchmark ARPU by game and platform.
- **Player Cohort Analysis & Retention:** Document new player cohorts' lifetime value (LTV) and retention curves. Healthy retention: 30-day retention >40%, 90-day >20%, 365-day >10%. Declining retention = churn = need for new content to retain players. Model player LTV under various retention scenarios.
- **Content Update Cadence & Impact:** Track major content update schedule and impact on DAU/ARPU. Major updates typically drive 20–50% DAU spikes temporarily. Assess whether [COMPANY] content pipeline is sufficient to maintain engagement long-term or if gaps between updates risk churn.

**C. Intellectual Property & Franchise Valuation**

**Role:** Assess IP franchise moat and long-term value.

- **Franchise Strength & Durability:** Assess IP portfolio: iconic franchises (Mario, Call of Duty, Grand Theft Auto, The Legend of Zelda) command pricing power and loyalty. Document franchise revenue contribution and trends. Aging franchises face renewal/reboot risk; dormant franchises can be revived (successful: Diablo, Final Fantasy).
- **Licensing & IP Expansion:** Document licensing revenue (mobile, merchandise, media rights). Strong IP enables spin-offs and expansion. Model revenue from Netflix adaptations, theatrical releases, or merchandise. IP licensing = non-core but high-margin revenue stream.
- **User-Generated Content & Community:** For platforms with UGC (Roblox, Fortnite, Minecraft), document % of revenue from creator economy and platform economics. Robust creator economy = engagement and retention driver; weak creator ecosystem = risk to growth.
- **Competitive IP Risk:** Monitor threat from competitor franchises and new IP launches. Disruptive new game could steal players and revenue (e.g., Fortnite disrupted Call of Duty market share 2018–2020). Assess [COMPANY]'s innovation rate and IP refresh cadence to defend against disruption.

**D. Financial Logic & Valuation**

**Role:** Apply gaming-specific valuation frameworks.

- **EV/Bookings Multiple:** For live service games, apply EV/annualized bookings multiple (typically 4–8x, higher for high-growth, lower for declining titles). Compare to historical range and peers. EV/Bookings typically lower than EV/Revenue due to bookings recurrence.
- **Earnings Quality & FCF Conversion:** Track % of bookings converting to GAAP revenue and FCF. Live service games have high incremental FCF margin (50–80%) as marginal player content is low-cost to produce. FCF margin > GAAP margin suggests quality earnings.
- **Multiple Revenue Streams Valuation:** For [COMPANY] with diverse revenue (games + subscription + licensing + esports), value each stream separately and blend. Subscription valued at SaaS multiple (8–12x EBITDA); licensing at lower multiple (3–5x); core games at game industry multiples.
- **Segment-Level Analysis:** Document revenue by segment (mobile, console, PC) and segment profitability. High-margin segments (mobile, PC) may have higher valuation multiple than console segment (lower margin). Compare [COMPANY]'s segment mix to peers.

**E. Pattern Matching & IDP Flagging**

**Role:** Identify critical risks and catalysts.

- **Bookings Deceleration:** Monitor bookings growth vs. GAAP revenue growth. If bookings decelerating faster than GAAP revenue, indicates forward demand weakness (deferred revenue declining). Red flag for 6–12 month forward GAAP revenue.
- **DAU Stalling or Declining:** Any sustained DAU decline in major franchises = churn risk. Major content updates can reverse DAU trends temporarily, but if updates don't drive DAU, indicates diminishing engagement and monetization ceiling.
- **Content Pipeline Risk:** Long gap between major content updates = DAU decline and churn risk. Monitor [COMPANY]'s content roadmap: are updates scheduled regularly or sparse? Sparse roadmap = execution risk.
- **Regulatory & Platform Risk:** App Store / Google Play policy changes (commission reduction, removal risk) impact mobile revenue. Regulatory scrutiny of loot boxes and gambling mechanics in games. Assess material impact on revenue and profitability.
- **Competitive Launch Risk:** Monitor competitor game launches. Major launches (Call of Duty, Diablo, Final Fantasy) in same genre can fragment player base and impact [COMPANY]'s player retention.

**F. Investigation Tracks**

**Track 1: Bookings Growth & Deferred Revenue Visibility**
- Calculate bookings from GAAP revenue and deferred revenue change: Bookings = GAAP Revenue + ending deferred revenue – beginning deferred revenue. Project forward bookings using current deferred revenue and expected GAAP revenue recognition timeline.
- Model deferred revenue trend: if deferred revenue declining, indicates softening demand for future content/passes. If stable/rising, indicates strength. Quantify % of bookings at risk if deferred revenue declines further.
- Compare bookings growth trajectory to historical rates and peer guidance. Any deceleration = demand warning signal.

**Track 2: Player Engagement & Retention Economics**
- Isolate DAU/MAU trends by major title. Which franchises growing? Which declining? Model total DAU / MAU for [COMPANY] and compare to last 8 quarters.
- Estimate total player base from DAU and estimate retention curves. Model player cohort LTV using retention rate assumptions and ARPU. Assess whether LTV > CAC (player acquisition cost) to justify continued investment.
- Identify "aging franchise" risk: titles with mature/stable DAU and declining ARPU = difficult to grow. Target these franchises for content refresh or sunsetting.

**Track 3: Content Pipeline & Franchise Renewal**
- Document major content release schedule for next 18–24 months. Identify gaps: months between major releases. Estimate DAU impact of each release (pullback to current level post-release if no update for 2+ months).
- Model ARPU trajectory: if new content improves cosmetics/battlepass sales, ARPU may rise; if content is free or cosmetics are boring, ARPU flat/declining.
- Assess IP renewal risk: any flagship franchise in need of reboot? Model revenue impact of successful reboot (+50% franchise revenue spike) vs. failed reboot (franchise discontinued). Probability-weight outcomes.
