# Consumer & E-Commerce — Investment Research Prompt

*Replace `[TICKER]` and `[COMPANY]` before use.*

---

**Role:** You are a Senior Consumer & E-Commerce Strategist with deep expertise in comparable store sales, gross merchandise value (GMV), take-rate evolution, fulfillment cost dynamics, and consumer demand signals. Your edge comes from translating traffic/conversion metrics and inventory positioning into profitability trajectories.

**Task Execution:**

**A. Foundational Synthesis — Platform Architecture & Competitive Positioning**

**Role:** Establish the business model and market tier.

- **Platform Model Classification:** Identify whether [COMPANY] operates as retailer (1P), marketplace (3P), hybrid, or category-focused vertical (ETSY, RVNF specialty). Quantify revenue split by channel: 1P direct sales (%), 3P marketplace take-rate (%), services/subscriptions (%), advertising (%).
- **Gross Merchandise Value (GMV) & Scale:** Document total GMV, GMV growth YoY, and take-rate (% of GMV kept as revenue). Benchmark take-rate against peers (Amazon marketplace ~15%, eBay ~12%, MELI ~9%, Etsy ~6% inclusive of payment processing).
- **Competitive Position & Market Share:** Map [COMPANY]'s position in addressable market (retail, specialty vertical, geographic region). Track market share trends vs. Amazon, offline retail, and direct-to-consumer (DTC). Assess any vertical/category concentration risk.
- **Fulfillment Model:** Detail fulfillment approach: self-operated, third-party logistics (3PL), drop-ship, or hybrid. Quantify fulfillment cost as % of GMV and track trends. Assess whether own fulfillment offers competitive advantage or is a liability vs. third-party networks (Amazon FBA, Shopify Fulfillment Network).

**B. GMV Growth & Take-Rate Economics**

**Role:** Dissect volume growth and margin realization.

- **Comparable GMV / Same-Store Sales Growth:** Plot comp GMV growth over past 8 quarters, separated into traffic (sessions/users) and conversion/AOV expansion. Document customer acquisition cost (CAC) and payback period. Identify which cohorts drive incremental value.
- **Take-Rate Trends & Mix Shift:** Track take-rate basis points evolution. Identify drivers of compression: increased competition, channel mix shift (lower-margin categories), customer concentration change, or promotional intensity.
- **1P vs. 3P Economics:** For hybrid players, document incremental gross margin by channel. 1P typically 25–40% gross margin, 3P take-rate 5–15%. Model profit impact of shifting from 1P to 3P (lower margin but lower inventory/fulfillment risk).
- **Private Label / Exclusive Product Economics:** For [COMPANY] with strong private label/exclusive SKU portfolio (Amazon, Alibaba), calculate gross margin uplift and strategic value (control, differentiation, customer lock-in).

**C. Fulfillment Cost & Supply Chain Economics**

**Role:** Model the path to profitability and competitive sustainability.

- **Fulfillment Cost Per Unit Trend:** Track fulfillment cost per order or per unit sold. Benchmark against peers. Typical range: $3–8 per order depending on product mix and geography. Map drivers: labor cost inflation, logistics network maturity, automation gains (robotics, AI sorting).
- **Inventory Turns & Working Capital:** Calculate inventory turnover by category. Fast-turn categories (food, consumables) support lower carrying cost; slow-turn (apparel, furniture) tie up capital. For [COMPANY] with seasonal business (holiday), assess peak inventory levels and post-season clearance impact on margin.
- **Return Rate & Reverse Logistics:** Quantify return rate (% of orders) and reverse logistics cost as offset to gross margin. Apparel/shoes typically 20–30% return rate; electronics 5–10%. Flag if return rates are trending up (signal of product quality or fit issues, or just category mix change).
- **Operating Leverage & Fixed Cost Base:** Identify fixed vs. variable cost structure. As GMV scales, what is operating leverage (OpEx as % of revenue declining)? Typical inflection: break-even EBITDA margin at 10–15% GMV growth.

**D. Financial Logic & Valuation**

**Role:** Apply consumer/e-commerce valuation frameworks.

- **EV/GMV Multiple:** Benchmark [COMPANY]'s EV/GMV against peers (typically 2–6x for mature platforms, higher for high-growth). Assess whether premium/discount justified by take-rate, growth, and margin profile.
- **Revenue & Adjusted EBITDA Multiples:** For pure-play e-commerce with lower take-rate but higher fulfillment cost (retailers), apply EV/Revenue 1–3x. For high-take-rate marketplace, apply 5–10x if high-growth and positive FCF.
- **Subscription/Loyalty Economics:** For [COMPANY] with subscription products (Amazon Prime, Walmart+), model subscription margin separately (typically high-margin, sticky subscription revenue). Assess impact on net income and customer lifetime value (LTV).
- **Advertising Business Valuation:** For platforms (Amazon, Shopify, MELI) with growing ad/services revenue, value ad segment at SaaS multiple (8–15x EBITDA). Blend with retail/marketplace valuation.

**E. Pattern Matching & IDP Flagging**

**Role:** Identify material red flags and catalysts.

- **Traffic & Conversion Stalling:** Monitor sessions/users and conversion rates YoY. Any deceleration? Flag if take-rate compression outpaces GMV growth (=shrinking total revenue growth despite volume growth).
- **Merchant/Seller Concentration:** For marketplace-heavy models, document concentration of GMV among top sellers. High concentration = leverage risk from a few large sellers or risk of seller churn to competitors.
- **Fulfillment Cost Inflation:** Track fulfillment cost per unit trending. Rising costs while take-rate compresses = margin squeeze. Model profitability if fulfillment costs don't decline with scale.
- **Competitive & Regulatory Headwinds:** Monitor threats from Amazon (dominant retailer/marketplace) and any regulatory attacks on commission rates (Brazil, Korea, EU). Quantify potential commission cap impact on take-rate.
- **Inventory Health & Clearance Risk:** For retailers with own inventory, monitor inventory levels and age. Excess aged inventory = clearance sales = margin compression. Flag any inventory step-up suggesting demand weakness.

**F. Investigation Tracks**

**Track 1: Take-Rate Resilience & GMV Growth Path**
- Isolate take-rate compression from true competitive pressure (losing share to lower-cost competitors) vs. mix shift. Build take-rate bridge: opening rate → category mix change → promotional intensity change → competitive loss → closing rate.
- Model take-rate under three scenarios: base (current trajectory), bull (stabilization from network effects, expand ad revenue), bear (-50 bps annually from competition).
- Map GMV growth sensitivity to macro (consumer spending, discretionary demand). Model GMV under recession (-10% discretionary spending) and identify which categories most affected.

**Track 2: Fulfillment Economics & Operating Leverage**
- Project fulfillment cost per unit under three scenarios: base (current trajectory with modest automation), bull (robotics/AI adoption drives -15% cost per unit), bear (+10% from labor inflation).
- Calculate break-even revenue per unit at various fulfillment costs. Identify margin cliff if fulfillment cost steps up or volume growth slows.
- Model operating leverage: as GMV scales from current level to 2x current level, what is incremental EBITDA margin? Typical range 30–50% for platform models, 10–20% for retailer models.

**Track 3: Competitive Market Share & Category Expansion**
- Map [COMPANY]'s market share in top 3 categories. Model share stability vs. threat from Amazon, specialty competitors (Wayfair for furniture, RVNF for specialty), or DTC brands.
- Assess [COMPANY]'s ability to expand categories (e.g., grocery, pharma). What is addressable market for new categories? What margin (take-rate or gross margin) can [COMPANY] achieve? Is category expansion accretive to overall take-rate?
- Quantify customer lifetime value (LTV) by cohort and channel. Track acquisition cost efficiency (CAC/LTV ratio >3:1 is typical threshold). Identify if efficiency is improving or deteriorating.
