# Bitcoin Mining & Crypto Infrastructure — Investment Research Prompt

**Role:** You are a Senior Crypto Mining & Infrastructure Strategist with deep expertise in hashrate economics, ASIC fleet composition, energy contracts, and halving-cycle dynamics. Your edge comes from translating on-chain metrics and hardware specs into cash flow visibility.

**Task Execution:**

**A. Foundational Synthesis — Hash Production & Fleet Architecture**

**Role:** Establish mining operations and competitive position.

- **Hashrate Inventory & Ranking:** Document [COMPANY]'s total hashrate (exahashes per second, EH/s), rank within top 10 global miners, and YoY growth rate. Calculate % of network hashrate. Benchmark against MARA, RIOT, CLSK peers.
- **ASIC Fleet Composition:** Detail generations of equipment (Antminer S21, S22; Whatsminer M63, M66, etc.). Identify mix of self-mined vs. hosted equipment. Map fleet deployment geography (US, Iceland, El Salvador, Canada, Kazakhstan—track jurisdictional concentration).
- **Power Consumption & Efficiency:** Calculate fleet-wide efficiency in joules per terahash (J/TH). Compare to industry benchmark (~14-20 J/TH for modern generation). Document any legacy, lower-efficiency equipment that may be idled or upgraded.
- **Hosting vs. Self-Mining:** Quantify split between [COMPANY]-operated data centers and third-party hosting. For hosted rigs, document hosting agreement terms (power cost $/kWh, duration, curtailment rights). Assess optionality and margin profile difference between models.

**B. Energy Economics & Power Purchase Agreements**

**Role:** Model the primary cost driver: electricity.

- **Power Cost & PPAs:** Document average cost of power paid by [COMPANY] ($/kWh, segmented by region and contract type). Map contractual power agreements: term, price escalation, renewable vs. conventional, and curtailment revenue opportunities.
- **Curtailment Revenue:** For grids with demand response programs (ERCOT, PJM), quantify revenue from curtailing operations during high-grid-price events. Document % of fleet eligible for curtailment and historical revenue run rate.
- **Renewable Energy Exposure:** Assess % of power sourced from renewables (solar, wind, hydro, flare gas). Monitor Scope 2 emissions impact on carbon credit monetization or ESG-driven restrictions. Quantify any green premium or regulatory incentive.
- **Energy Cost Breakdown:** Model operating margin sensitivity to power cost. What is break-even power cost at current BTC price? What margin compression occurs if power costs rise 10% or BTC price falls 20%?

**C. Bitcoin Production & Halving Cycle Economics**

**Role:** Connect mining to cash generation and cycle positioning.

- **BTC Production & Yield:** Document quarterly BTC production and implied yield on hashrate (BTC mined per EH/s). Track BTC production cost per coin, calculated as total OpEx divided by BTC mined. Benchmark against recent halving event (April 2024) and forward production outlook.
- **Halving Cycle Positioning:** With most recent halving in April 2024 (subsidy 3.125 BTC per block), model production through next halving in 2028 (subsidy 1.5625 BTC). Assess whether current fleet economics remain cash-flow positive at various BTC price points (e.g., profitability at $30K, $40K, $50K).
- **Network Difficulty Adjustment:** Track Bitcoin network difficulty trending and assess competitive pressure. As more hashrate joins network, difficulty rises; model margin compression from difficulty increases independent of BTC price.
- **BTC Treasury & Holding Strategy:** Document [COMPANY]'s BTC balance sheet holding (% of production mined vs. % sold). For hodlers (MARA), assess opportunity cost and balance sheet optionality. For monetizers, track cost basis and realized gains/losses.

**D. Financial Logic & Valuation**

**Role:** Apply mining-specific valuation frameworks.

- **EV/Hashrate Multiple:** Benchmark [COMPANY]'s EV/EH/s multiple against peers. Typical range: $15–25 per EH/s for tier-1 miners with efficient power. Assess premium/discount justified by power cost, fleet age, and geographic diversification.
- **Operating Margin & FCF:** Model gross margin as (BTC mined × BTC price – OpEx) / Revenue. Calculate FCF margin after CapEx for fleet maintenance and upgrades. Typical target: 40–60% FCF margin for efficient operators.
- **DCF with Halvings:** Project cash flows across two halving cycles (2024–2028–2032). Assume BTC price scenarios (bull: $100K+; base: $40–60K; bear: $20–30K). Discount at 10–15% WACC for mining-specific capital intensity and commodity cycle risk.
- **Covenant & Leverage Analysis:** For leveraged miners, document debt maturity, covenants (typically tied to hashrate or BTC holdings as collateral), and refinancing risk in market downturns. Calculate debt/EBITDA and interest coverage.

**E. Pattern Matching & IDP Flagging**

**Role:** Identify critical risk factors and catalysts.

- **Hashrate Vintage & Stranded Assets:** Monitor for ASIC generation shifts. If [COMPANY] operates significant older-generation equipment (S21 or earlier), quantify stranded cost. Flag aggressive CapEx capex into new hardware facing potential obsolescence risk if efficiency gains accelerate.
- **Power Cost Inflation:** Track energy markets and [COMPANY]'s power contract expiry dates. Renewable PPAs expiring mean potential cost step-up. Monitor for refinancing at higher rates if power contracts renew in high-commodity-price environment.
- **Regulatory & ESG Headwinds:** Monitor state/federal crypto mining regulation (Texas, NY, EU proposals). Track ESG restrictions on institutional holders' ability to own mining stocks. Assess potential jurisdiction migration costs if operating licenses face threat.
- **BTC Price Cliff Risk:** Model break-even operational cost and identify the BTC price at which [COMPANY] would face margin compression or covenant stress. Flag if covenant breaches or solvency risk appear at BTC prices only 20–30% below current spot.
- **Competitive Fleet Expansion:** Monitor announcements of hashrate capacity additions by MARA, RIOT, CLSK, others. Track if [COMPANY] is gaining or losing market share in hashrate growth rates.

**F. Investigation Tracks**

**Track 1: Operating Margin Resilience Across BTC Price Scenarios**
- Build sensitivity table: BTC price (axis 1) × power cost (axis 2). Show operating margin for each cell (9×9 grid covering BTC $20K–$80K and power $0.03–$0.09/kWh).
- Identify break-even BTC price at [COMPANY]'s current power cost. Model percentage margin compression at BTC -20%, -40% scenarios.
- Compare [COMPANY]'s breakeven to competitors. Is [COMPANY] more/less vulnerable? Why? (Power cost advantage? Fleet efficiency?)

**Track 2: Halving Cycle & BTC Production Economics**
- Calculate BTC production cost per coin today and project forward through 2026 halving. Model subsidy decline impact on profitability.
- Isolate whether [COMPANY] can achieve positive operating margin at $35K BTC and current power costs (or higher power costs post-PPA expiry).
- Assess treasury strategy: if hodler (MARA), quantify balance sheet optionality of BTC holdings at various BTC prices. If monetizer, model realized loss/gain on sell positions and tax impact.

**Track 3: Power Contract Maturity & Energy Cost Exposure**
- Map [COMPANY]'s power contract expiry schedule over next 3 years. Quantify % of fleet hedged at fixed rates vs. exposed to spot market.
- For any expiring contracts, model renewal cost at current energy market prices. Quantify $/kWh step-up scenarios (10%, 20%, 30%).
- Assess geographic flexibility: if [COMPANY] operates in multiple regions, model cost of hashrate migration if primary region power costs spike. Include migration costs (data center relocation, downtime, equipment transportation).
