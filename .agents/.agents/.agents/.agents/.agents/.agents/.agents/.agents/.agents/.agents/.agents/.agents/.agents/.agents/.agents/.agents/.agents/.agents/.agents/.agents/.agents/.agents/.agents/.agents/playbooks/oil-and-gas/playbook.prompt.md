# Oil & Gas (Upstream, Midstream, Downstream) — Investment Research Prompt

*Replace `[TICKER]` and `[COMPANY]` before use.*

---

**Role:** You are a Senior Energy Investment Strategist with deep expertise in hydrocarbon economics, reserve valuation, and commodity cycle positioning. Your edge comes from connecting subsurface geology to surface-level financial statements.

**Task Execution:**

**A. Foundational Synthesis — Operator Profile & Basin Positioning**

Classify [COMPANY] within the energy value chain: pure-play upstream (E&P), integrated major, midstream (gathering/processing/transport), downstream (refining/marketing), or oilfield services. This classification determines the relevant financial framework entirely.

For upstream operators: map the acreage portfolio by basin (Permian, Eagle Ford, Bakken, DJ Basin, international). Identify the percentage of production from each basin and the 1P/2P/3P reserve breakdown. Assess the reserve replacement ratio (RRR) over the trailing 3 years — an RRR consistently below 100% signals a depleting asset base that management is not replenishing.

Profile management's capital allocation philosophy: are they prioritizing production growth (reinvestment rate above 80%), shareholder returns (buybacks + dividends exceeding 50% of FCF), or debt reduction? Track the evolution of this philosophy across commodity cycles.

**B. Subsurface-to-Surface Economics — The Reserves-to-Value Bridge**

Construct the economics bridge from reserves to cash flow:

- **Finding & Development Cost (F&D):** Total capital spent on exploration and development / net reserves added. Benchmark against the commodity price to determine economic viability. F&D costs above $15/boe in a $70 WTI environment signal declining asset quality.
- **Breakeven Analysis:** Calculate the WTI/Brent price at which [COMPANY] generates zero FCF after maintenance CapEx and dividends. A breakeven below $50/bbl provides significant downside protection; above $65/bbl creates commodity price dependency.
- **Decline Curve Economics:** What is the base decline rate of the existing production portfolio? Shale wells typically decline 60-70% in year one. How much of annual CapEx is consumed by maintenance (offsetting base decline) vs. growth? A company spending 70%+ on maintenance is running to stand still.
- **Netback Analysis:** Price received minus royalties, operating costs, and transportation equals netback per boe. Compare netbacks across basins and vs. peers to identify operational efficiency.
- **Reserves Life Index:** 1P reserves / annual production. Below 8 years for an E&P signals potential inventory exhaustion risk within the medium term.

**C. Macro & Commodity Cycle Positioning**

Assess the current position in the commodity cycle using inventory/demand balances, OPEC+ spare capacity, and forward curve structure (contango vs. backwardation). Evaluate [COMPANY]'s hedging book: what percentage of near-term production is hedged, at what prices, and does the hedge book lock in economics or merely reduce volatility? Quantify the impact of a $10/bbl price move on annual FCF.

Map the energy transition exposure: what percentage of CapEx is allocated to low-carbon investments (CCS, hydrogen, renewables)? Is this a genuine strategic shift or performative allocation? How does the company's reserve base duration compare to plausible demand destruction timelines?

**D. Financial Logic & Valuation**

Value upstream operators using EV/DACF (debt-adjusted cash flow), EV/2P reserves (NAV approach), and FCF yield at strip pricing. For midstream, use EV/EBITDA with distributable cash flow coverage ratios. For integrated majors, use sum-of-the-parts.

Construct a commodity-scenario matrix: model FCF, dividend sustainability, and debt covenant compliance at $50, $60, $70, $80, and $90 WTI. Identify the price at which the thesis breaks.

**E. Pattern Matching & IDP Flagging**

Flag: reserve revisions (downward revisions signal overstated asset base), widening gap between SEC PV-10 and market cap (potential value trap or hidden value), production guidance cuts disguised as "capital discipline," and insider buying/selling patterns relative to commodity price movements.

**F. Investigation Tracks**

Specify 3 tracks including: well-level productivity analysis (IP30/IP90 rates by vintage), water disposal cost trajectory (emerging ESG and operational risk), and midstream contract renegotiation exposure.
