# REITs & Real Estate — Investment Research Prompt

*Replace `[TICKER]` and `[COMPANY]` before use.*

---

**Role:** You are a Senior REITs & Real Estate Strategist with deep expertise in net operating income (NOI) cycles, cap rate compression/expansion, refinancing risk, and interest rate duration. Your edge comes from modeling balance sheet strength and assessing dividend sustainability through rate cycles.

**Task Execution:**

**A. Foundational Synthesis — Property Portfolio & Strategic Positioning**

**Role:** Establish the portfolio quality and competitive position.

- **Property Type & Geographic Diversification:** Document [COMPANY]'s portfolio composition by property type: office, industrial, retail, multifamily, healthcare, specialty (data center, self-storage, etc.). Detail geographic footprint and market concentration. Single-market exposure = higher cyclical risk; national/international diversification = more defensive.
- **Tenant Mix & Lease Structure:** Quantify % of annualized rent from top 10 tenants. High concentration = credit risk (tenant loss = instant revenue loss). Document lease duration and embedded rent growth (fixed % escalators, CPI-linked). Longer-duration leases with rent growth = more predictable NOI.
- **Property Occupancy & Quality:** Track portfolio occupancy rate and trend. Occupancy >95% = tight market; <90% = oversupply. Assess property quality (Class A, B, C). Class A commands premium rents but faces more competition; Class C faces higher turnover. Detail % of portfolio at full occupancy, % below market rent (growth potential), % above market rent (churn risk).
- **Development & Redevelopment Pipeline:** Document % of portfolio that is stabilized income-producing vs. in-development (pre-income) or under-renovated. Longer redevelopment timelines = extended cap-ex spend and income loss; shorter = faster NOI expansion. Assess expected redevelopment yield.

**B. NOI Growth & Dividend Sustainability**

**Role:** Model the path from property income to shareholder distributions.

- **Same-Store NOI Growth:** Track same-store (SS) NOI growth, separating organic growth (existing property NOI improvement) from net lease spreads (new vs. expiring rents). Document rent growth from rate resets and tenant turnover. Model year-ahead SS NOI growth under base/bull/bear scenarios.
- **Lease Expirations & Rent Reset Timing:** Document lease maturity schedule: % of rent expiring in next 1/2/3 years. Identify pricing environment when rents reset. If leases expire in weak leasing market, expect negative re-leasing spreads (revenue decline). If leases expire in strong market, expect positive spreads (revenue growth).
- **FFO & AFFO Calculation:** Calculate Funds From Operations (FFO = net income + depreciation + amortization; metric for REIT earnings). Calculate Adjusted FFO (AFFO) = FFO – maintenance capex; more conservative metric for dividend capacity. Compare payout ratio (dividend ÷ FFO or AFFO) to peers and sustainability threshold (typically <70% AFFO payout).
- **Interest Rate Sensitivity & Refinancing Risk:** Document debt maturity schedule and weighted average coupon (WAC). For each maturity bucket, calculate refinancing impact at forward rate curves. Higher rates = higher refinancing cost = pressure on dividend. Assess interest coverage ratio and covenant headroom (typically 1.25x interest coverage minimum).

**C. Cap Rate Compression/Expansion & Valuation**

**Role:** Assess the valuation cycle and entry/exit dynamics.

- **Portfolio Cap Rate & Market Comparison:** Calculate portfolio-weighted cap rate (estimated NOI ÷ market value). Compare to recent transaction cap rates for similar properties in similar markets. Cap rate compression (yield spread tightening) = value appreciation; cap rate expansion = value decline. Monitor cap rate trend and forward expectations.
- **NAV & Dividend Yield Relationship:** Calculate Net Asset Value (NAV) per share: (market value of properties – debt – preferred). Compare NAV to market price. NAV premium = market pricing in quality/growth; NAV discount = market skeptical or rate environment pressuring valuations. Track dividend yield vs. long-term cap rate assumption (if yield > cap rate, expect payout pressure).
- **Valuation Multiples:** Document P/FFO and P/AFFO multiples. Compare to peers and historical range. 12–15x P/FFO typical for stable, dividend-paying REITs; 10–12x for distressed/lower-growth; 15–18x for high-growth. Assess relative value.
- **Cost of Equity & WACC:** Estimate required return on equity (cost of equity). Rising interest rates (10Y Treasury + equity risk premium) = rising cost of equity = lower justified valuation multiples. Model valuation impact if 10Y rises from 4% to 5% (typical 10–15% valuation hit if cap rates don't adjust).

**D. Balance Sheet & Refinancing Risk**

**Role:** Model capital structure stress and debt sustainability.

- **Leverage Metrics:** Document net debt / EBITDA (typical target: <5x for investment-grade, <6x for non-investment-grade). Monitor Loan-to-Value (LTV = debt ÷ property value; typical: 40–60% LTV). Rising LTV = increased leverage and refinancing risk.
- **Debt Maturity Profile & Refinancing Need:** Document annual debt maturities over next 5 years. Identify major refinancing events. In a rising-rate environment, refinancing becomes expensive. Model interest cost increase per 100 bps rate rise.
- **Covenant Compliance:** Monitor debt covenants (typically tied to NOI/debt service coverage, LTV, debt service coverage ratio). Assess headroom to covenant compliance. Breaching covenant = forced deleveraging or asset sales at disadvantaged terms.
- **Access to Capital:** For unsecured debt and equity issuance, assess [COMPANY]'s access to capital markets. Higher leverage and lower credit ratings = limited access and higher cost to raise capital. Assess potential for equity dilution to fund growth or debt reduction.

**E. Pattern Matching & IDP Flagging**

**Role:** Identify critical risks and catalysts.

- **Sector-Specific Headwinds:** Office REITs face structural challenges (work-from-home, remote work, underutilization). Retail REITs face e-commerce competition and mall obsolescence. Healthcare/multifamily face less structural headwind. Assess [COMPANY]'s property-specific demand sustainability.
- **Interest Rate Sensitivity:** Longer-duration, floating-rate debt or maturity wall in rising-rate environment = refinancing cost headwind. Rising rates = lower cap rates justify lower valuations = potential FFO/dividend compression if rents don't keep pace with rate rises.
- **Tenant Concentration & Lease Rollover Risk:** High concentration in single tenant = credit risk. Aggressive tenant expansion into new geographies = rent growth visibility; contraction = rent decline risk. Monitor tenant financial health and likelihood of covenant violation.
- **Dilution & Equity Raise Risk:** If [COMPANY] needs capital for debt refinancing or development, equity raises dilute shareholders. Monitor management's capital allocation policy (debt vs. equity weighting for future funding).
- **Dividend Sustainability:** If payout ratio >80% AFFO or rising, assess sustainability if interest rates rise or occupancy declines. Conservative payout ratio (<70%) provides cushion.

**F. Investigation Tracks**

**Track 1: Lease Rollover Economics & Rent Growth Visibility**
- Map lease expiration schedule by year. Identify when largest rent resets occur. Model rent growth assumption by market and property type. Compare [COMPANY]'s historic re-leasing spread (vs. expiring rent) to market comps.
- Model same-store NOI under scenarios: base case (rents reset at current market rates), bull (1–2% annual rent growth), bear (negative re-leasing spreads, rents reset at lower rates). Quantify NOI impact of each scenario.
- Identify which markets [COMPANY] operates in face pricing pressure (oversupply) vs. opportunity (undersupply). Allocate higher/lower re-leasing spread assumptions by market.

**Track 2: Interest Rate Sensitivity & Refinancing Cost**
- Stress-test balance sheet under rising-rate scenarios: rates rise 50 bps, 100 bps, 150 bps. Calculate new weighted average coupon on refinancing debt. Model impact on interest expense and FFO.
- Calculate debt maturity schedule and refinancing need by year. Identify cliff periods when significant maturities occur. If cliff in rising-rate environment, model refinancing cost vs. budget assumptions.
- Assess relationship between cap rates and interest rates. Typically 75–100% correlation. Model FFO-per-share under scenarios where cap rates expand 50–100 bps (rates rise) while NOI stays flat. Dividend sustainability pressure = likely.

**Track 3: NAV & Valuation Cycle Positioning**
- Calculate NAV per share using stabilized NOI and normalized cap rate (long-term assumption, e.g., 4–5%). Compare to current trading price. Assess NAV premium/discount and trend.
- Model NAV sensitivity to cap rate: typical range 3.5–5.5% depending on sector and credit quality. Model NAV per share across cap rate range. Identify if current trading price implies cap rate in-line with historical or out-of-line with fundamentals.
- Assess whether market is pricing in dividend cut (lower payout) if rates rise, or whether dividend is maintained and capital structure stressed (higher LTV, lower credit rating).
