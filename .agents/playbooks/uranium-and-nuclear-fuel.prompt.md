**B. Supply/Demand Fundamentals — The Nuclear Fuel Balance Sheet**

**B1. Supply Architecture**

Decompose annual supply into:
- **Primary mine production**: tonnage (Mlbs U3O8), YoY growth, geographic concentration, top producers (Kazakhstan ~43%, Canada ~15%, Namibia ~11%, Australia ~10%), cost curve (cash cost distribution)
- **Secondary supply**: government/military stockpile drawdowns (Megatons to Megawatts legacy), underfeeding by enrichers, MOX fuel recycling, commercial inventory liquidation
- **Conversion & enrichment capacity**: utilization rates, planned expansions, Russian share of global SWU capacity (~46%)

Assess supply elasticity: how responsive is new mine production to price increases? Lead time from discovery to production (10-15 years for greenfield; 3-5 years for ISL restart). Current capex cycle: underinvestment since Fukushima (2011) creating structural supply gap.

**B2. Demand Decomposition**

Break demand into:
- **Existing reactor fleet**: number of operating reactors globally (~440), annual fuel loading requirements (~180 Mlbs U3O8/year), capacity factor trends, fleet age and life extension programs
- **New reactor construction**: reactors under construction (~60), planned (~110), proposed (~350). Pipeline by country (China, India, Turkey, Egypt, UK, Poland, Czech Republic)
- **SMR (Small Modular Reactor) demand**: deployment timelines, fuel type requirements (LEU vs. HALEU), incremental demand per unit
- **HALEU demand**: Advanced reactors (Kairos, X-energy, TerraPower, Oklo) requiring 5-20% enriched uranium. Only Centrus Energy currently licensed for US HALEU production.
- **Contract vs. spot market**: percentage of utility procurement under long-term contract (~85%) vs. spot (~15%). Contract price vs. spot price dynamics.

Calculate the supply/demand deficit or surplus for trailing 3 years and forward 5-year estimate. Include secondary supply drawdown trajectory.

**B3. Inventory & Contracting Dynamics**

Map identifiable inventories:
- Utility inventories (average months of forward coverage — historically 24-36 months, currently declining)
- Government strategic reserves (US DOE stockpile, Russian state inventory)
- Physical trust holdings (Sprott, Yellow Cake — effectively removing supply from market)
- Enricher and converter working inventory
- Secondary supply depletion timeline

The uncovered requirements ratio (percentage of forward demand not yet contracted) is the critical demand indicator. Rising uncovered requirements force utilities into contract cycles, driving term price escalation.

**C. Macro Regime & Policy Driver Analysis**

**C1. Energy Policy & Nuclear Renaissance**

Map the global nuclear policy landscape:
- **Pro-nuclear policy shifts**: countries adding nuclear to clean energy taxonomy (EU, Japan restart, South Korea reversal, US bipartisan support)
- **COP28/29 commitments**: Triple nuclear capacity by 2050 pledge (signed by 20+ countries)

**C2. Geopolitical & Supply Chain Risk**

Assess geopolitical dimensions:
- **Russia dependency**: Rosatom controls ~46% of global enrichment (SWU), ~30% of conversion. Sanctions risk, ban timeline (US ban on Russian LEU effective 2028, with waivers)
- **Kazakhstan risk**: Kazatomprom (43% of global mine production) — geopolitical alignment (Russia/China influence), production guidance, state ownership
- **Western supply chain rebuilding**: US/EU/Canada efforts to onshore enrichment, conversion, fuel fabrication. Centrus HALEU cascade. URENCO expansion.
- **Sanctions & trade policy**: tariffs on Russian uranium, Section 232 investigations, TENEX (Russian supplier) contract status

**C3. Technology & Reactor Evolution**

Assess reactor technology impact on fuel demand:
- **Generation III+ fleet** (AP1000, EPR, VVER, Hualong One): standardized LEU demand, 60-80 year operating life
- **SMR deployment** (NuScale, GE-Hitachi BWRX-300, Rolls-Royce): timeline, incremental demand per unit (lower per reactor but potentially higher aggregate if deployed at scale)
- **Advanced/Gen IV reactors** (Kairos FHR, TerraPower Natrium, X-energy Xe-100): HALEU requirement, deployment timeline (2030+)
- **Fusion timeline**: ITER, Commonwealth Fusion, Type One Energy — not a demand factor before 2040 but narrative overhang

**D. Financial Logic & Valuation**

Uranium does not generate cash flows as a commodity. Value using:
- **Marginal cost of production analysis**: Incentive price to bring new Tier 1 production online (~$65-75/lb for greenfield conventional; ~$50-60 for ISL restart). 90th percentile cash cost as price floor.
- **Replacement cost analysis**: Cost to replace secondary supply depletion with primary production. As government stockpiles exhaust, primary production must fill the gap at higher marginal costs.
- **Contract price vs. spot price**: Long-term contract prices (Ux term indicator) vs. spot price (Ux/Numerco spot). Contract premiums signal utility urgency.
- **Historical price regime analysis**: Real (inflation-adjusted) price relative to prior cycles (2007 peak at $137/lb, Fukushima trough at $18/lb, 2024 recovery)
- **Inventory-adjusted supply/demand model**: Project forward supply/demand with secondary supply depletion. Model the year when uncovered requirements force aggressive contracting.
- **Positioning-based analysis**: Physical trust purchases (Sprott accumulation), ETF flows, speculative positioning in futures market.

Construct price scenario matrix at current spot, $80, $100, $120, and stress case ($50). Identify the price at which the investment thesis breaks.

**E. Pattern Matching & IDP Flagging**

Flag the following divergence patterns as IDPs:
1. **Spot price rising while term contract price is flat** — suggests speculative/financial buying, not utility procurement. Potentially fragile.
2. **Utility uncovered requirements increasing while inventories decline** — leading indicator of forced contracting cycle. Structurally bullish.
3. **Physical trust accumulation accelerating** — Sprott/Yellow Cake removing pounds from spot market, amplifying supply tightness.
4. **New reactor announcements exceeding construction starts** — pipeline inflation without execution. Demand may be overstated.
5. **Enrichment/conversion bottleneck tightening** — SWU prices rising faster than U3O8 spot, indicating fuel cycle constraint has shifted from mining to processing.
6. **Russia sanctions escalation** — any tightening of enrichment sanctions compresses available SWU supply by up to 46%, forcing emergency procurement.
7. **Mine production missing guidance** — Kazatomprom or Cameco production shortfalls compound deficit. Monitor quarterly production reports vs. guidance.
8. **AI/data center nuclear PPA announcements** — each PPA represents 10-20 year demand visibility for 1-2 reactors' worth of fuel. Track frequency and MW contracted.
