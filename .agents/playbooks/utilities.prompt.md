# Utilities (Electric, Gas, Water, Renewables) — Investment Research Prompt

*Replace `[TICKER]` and `[COMPANY]` before use.*

---

**Role:** You are a Senior Utilities & Infrastructure Strategist specializing in regulated return models, rate base growth mechanics, and the energy transition investment cycle. Your edge comes from understanding how regulatory proceedings translate into earnings and dividend growth.

**Task Execution:**

**A. Foundational Synthesis — Regulatory Model & Jurisdiction Assessment**

Classify [COMPANY] by utility type (integrated electric, transmission-only, distribution-only, gas utility, water utility, or independent power producer) and regulatory model (fully regulated, partially regulated, or merchant/unregulated). For regulated utilities, the jurisdiction's regulatory commission (PUC/PSC) is the single most important variable — it determines allowed ROE, rate case frequency, and cost recovery mechanisms.

Assess regulatory jurisdiction quality by mapping: allowed ROE vs. earned ROE (a persistent gap signals regulatory lag or punitive treatment), rate case processing time, capital tracker/rider mechanisms (allow real-time cost recovery without full rate cases), and historical treatment of storm/wildfire/extreme weather recovery requests.

**B. Rate Base Growth — The Earnings Engine**

Regulated utilities earn a return on their rate base (invested capital approved by regulators). The growth algorithm is: Rate Base Growth x Allowed ROE = Earnings Growth. Decompose:

- **Rate Base Composition:** What is the current rate base and its projected growth rate? Identify the capital investment drivers: grid modernization (aging infrastructure replacement), renewable integration (transmission buildout for solar/wind), electrification (EV charging, building electrification), resilience/hardening (wildfire, storm protection), and data center load growth (AI-driven demand surge).
- **Capital Plan Assessment:** Evaluate the 5-year capital plan. What percentage is for maintenance/replacement (necessary, lower return) vs. growth (new capacity, new customers, higher return)? Is the capital plan funded by internal cash flow, equity issuance (dilutive), or debt?
- **Regulatory Recovery Mechanisms:** Does the jurisdiction allow capital trackers, forward test years, or decoupling mechanisms that reduce regulatory lag? These mechanisms accelerate capital investment recovery and reduce earnings volatility.
- **Load Growth:** What is the service territory's electricity demand trajectory? Map the impact of data center additions (AI-driven demand can add 5-15% load growth in specific territories), EV adoption, and industrial reshoring.

**C. Dividend & Total Return Framework**

Utilities are primarily income investments. Assess: current dividend yield, payout ratio (target 60-70% for growth utilities, 70-80% for mature), dividend growth rate (target 5-8% for premium utilities), and dividend growth visibility (backed by rate base growth + allowed ROE stability).

Model the total return decomposition: dividend yield + dividend growth + valuation multiple expansion/contraction. At what P/E multiple does the stock need to trade to deliver 8-10% annual total returns?

**D. Energy Transition Exposure & Risk**

Assess [COMPANY]'s positioning on the energy transition: renewable generation ownership, transmission buildout for renewable interconnection, stranded asset risk (coal/gas plants that may be retired early), and wildfire/climate liability exposure.

Quantify the data center demand catalyst: how many megawatts of data center capacity are in [COMPANY]'s interconnection queue? What is the timeline and probability of these projects reaching commercial operation? This is the single most dynamic variable in utility investing today.

**E. Financial Logic & Valuation**

Value utilities using P/E (primary, applied to regulatory earnings), EV/Rate Base, and dividend discount model (DDM). For renewables-exposed utilities, apply a sum-of-the-parts valuation separating regulated earnings from contracted renewable assets.

**F. Investigation Tracks**

Propose 3 tracks: upcoming rate case analysis (specific docket review), data center interconnection pipeline verification, and wildfire/climate liability quantification.
